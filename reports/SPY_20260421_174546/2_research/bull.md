## Now let’s tackle the bear argument head-on

### Bear point 1: “RSI is overbought, so SPY is due for a pullback.”
My response: **Maybe a pullback, yes. Trend failure, no.**

An overbought RSI in isolation is not a sell signal. In a strong bull trend, RSI can stay elevated while price keeps grinding higher. The proper interpretation is:

- **New entries need more patience**
- **Existing positions should be held**
- **A pullback would be healthy, not fatal**

This is a bullish market, not a broken one.

---

### Bear point 2: “Geopolitical risk and oil spikes could derail the rally.”
This is the strongest bearish point — and still not enough to break the bull thesis.

Yes, the market is sensitive to oil and Middle East headlines. SPY even slipped about **0.4%** in a risk-off move tied to rising oil and renewed tension. But what matters is what happened next structurally:

- The market had already shown it could rally through fear
- The **Strait of Hormuz reopening** improved sentiment
- Broad equity strength remained intact
- The index is still above all key moving averages

So yes, headlines can create volatility. But volatility is not the same as a durable trend reversal.

If the bear wants to prove the thesis, they need to show that oil shock risk is translating into **persistent deterioration in trend, momentum, and breadth**. That has not happened.

---

### Bear point 3: “The rally is already extended.”
That’s true — but extension is not the same as exhaustion.

A market that’s extended above the 10 EMA after a sharp rebound can still keep rising. What we have here is a **strong V-shaped recovery**, and those often don’t end cleanly. They usually transition into one of two things:

1. another leg higher, or  
2. a consolidation that resets the move

Neither outcome is bearish for long-term investors.

If anything, the lesson is: **don’t chase blindly**, but also **don’t confuse a healthy, strong trend with a topping process**.

---

### Bear point 4: “This is just a recovery from a prior selloff.”
That would be concerning if price had stalled under key averages and momentum stayed weak. But that is not what’s happening.

Instead, SPY:

- reclaimed the **50 SMA**
- reclaimed the **200 SMA**
- pushed into **new high territory**

- maintained a rising long-term structure

That’s what durable recoveries look like before they become new uptrends.

## Lessons learned from setups like this

Here’s the key lesson from similar situations:  
**The mistake is often being too early with a bearish call just because a market is extended.**

I’ve seen traders repeatedly do this:
- see RSI over 70,
- assume “top,”
- short into a strong trend,

The better lesson is:

- and don’t let temporary headline risk override a confirmed bullish trend.
