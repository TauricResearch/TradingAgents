In momentum markets, extension can be a feature, not a flaw. The fact that price is holding well above the mid-band while MACD remains elevated is evidence of trend durability, not a reason to panic. The biggest upside opportunities usually come when markets stay “too strong” longer than cautious traders expect. You already own a position in a market that remains above all major trend references. Why voluntarily step out of a favorable regime just because the “ideal” entry has passed? That’s how traders turn winners into regret.

The upside still exists, but the immediate entry quality is not optimal. That’s exactly why hold beats buy here. Not because the market lacks opportunity, but because the opportunity is better harvested through existing exposure rather than through a new chase trade.

So I’d sharpen the conclusion like this: hold is the correct action, but not because SPY is weak or because upside is limited. Hold because the trend is still powerful, and the market has already done the hard work of confirming its strength.
