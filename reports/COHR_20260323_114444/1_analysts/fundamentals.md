# Comprehensive Fundamental Report: Coherent Corp. (COHR)
**Date of Analysis:** 2026-03-23

## Company Profile
Coherent Corp. operates in the Technology sector, specifically within the Scientific & Technical Instruments industry. The company is a prominent player in the laser and photonics market, providing engineered materials, lasers, and optoelectronic solutions for various applications including telecommunications, industrial manufacturing, and aerospace.

## Financial Statement Analysis

### Income Statement
Coherent has demonstrated consistent top-line revenue growth over the last five quarters (from $1.43B to $1.69B). However, profitability has been inconsistent. The company experienced a net loss in the quarter ending 2025-06-30 ($95.6M loss), driven by unusual items and high restructuring costs. While net income has recovered since then ($146.7M in Q4 2025), net margins remain thin at 4.66%.

### Balance Sheet
The company's liquidity position appears stable, with a Current Ratio of 2.25, indicating the ability to cover short-term liabilities. However, the capital structure is heavily leveraged, with a Debt-to-Equity ratio of 39.90. Total Debt stands at approximately $3.55B. Investors should monitor this debt load, especially given the interest expense burden reflected in the income statement.

### Cash Flow
Free Cash Flow (FCF) has been deteriorating in recent quarters, turning negative in the second half of 2025 (-$95.7M for the quarter ending 2025-12-31). This is partly due to significant capital expenditures (e.g., $153.6M in Q4 2025) and changes in working capital (specifically inventory buildup). Consistent negative FCF in an environment of high debt is a primary area of concern for long-term sustainability.

## Actionable Insights & Risks

### Risks
1.  **High Financial Leverage:** The debt-to-equity ratio and consistent interest expenses place pressure on net income and cash flow, especially if market conditions tighten.
2.  **Cash Flow Volatility:** The recent trend of negative FCF, driven by inventory accumulation and ongoing capital investment, reduces the company's financial flexibility.
3.  **High Volatility:** With a Beta of 1.91, COHR is significantly more volatile than the market, making it prone to sharp price swings, particularly following earnings reports.
4.  **Earnings Quality:** The wide gap between TTM P/E (251) and Forward P/E (34) suggests that the market is pricing in a massive turnaround in earnings growth. Any failure to hit these forward EPS projections could trigger significant downward pressure on the stock.

### Insights for Traders
*   **Monitor Debt Management:** Watch for any signs of debt reduction or refinancing initiatives in upcoming quarterly filings.
*   **Revenue vs. Profit:** While revenue growth is healthy, traders should prioritize improvements in profit margins and a return to positive Free Cash Flow as key catalysts for sustained price appreciation.
*   **Entry/Exit:** Given the high beta, consider utilizing technical indicators to time entries to avoid buying during peak volatility, especially ahead of quarterly releases.
