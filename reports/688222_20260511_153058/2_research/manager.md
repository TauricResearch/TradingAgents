**Recommendation**: Underweight

**Rationale**: This was a genuinely close and hard-fought debate, with both sides landing heavy blows. But when I weigh the arguments that survived the full exchange, the bear carried the day on the issues that matter most.

The bull's strongest card is the genuine uniqueness of DEL technology and the fact that the founding team is not selling. Those are real, and they provide a floor under the stock. The bull also correctly notes that the 3.51% reduction is finite, digestible over months, and partly routed through block trades that attract institutional counterparties.

But the bear landed three arguments that the bull could never satisfactorily rebut:

**First, the synchronized shareholder exit at depressed prices.** The bull calls it "normal PE/VC fund lifecycle." Normal would be one fund exiting. Two major shareholders filing nearly simultaneously when the stock is down 47% from its highs is not normal — it is a coordination of negative sentiment that demands an explanation the bull never provided. A rational fund manager with confidence in the business would seek an extension, not rush the exit at fire-sale prices.

**Second, the six-year failure to deliver on the license-out growth narrative.** This is the most damaging blow. The bull hangs the entire "option value" thesis on a single 2025 upfront payment deal, then asks for 2-3 more years of patience. But the company has been public since 2020. Six years. Zero meaningful milestone payments or royalty income in the financials. At some point, a story that never converts stops being an "option" and starts being a "lottery ticket." The bull never bridged this gap.

**Third, the AI strategic vacuum.** The bull argues DEL data is essential AI training material. That may be true. But the bear's counter — that management has articulated no AI partnership or integration strategy — went unanswered. A company sitting on supposedly priceless AI-relevant data but doing nothing visible to monetize that position is not a bullish signal; it's a red flag.

The bull did score points: the founding team's continued holding is meaningful, the stock has found a floor near 26-27 yuan, and the DEL platform is genuinely world-class. These prevent me from going to Sell. But they do not outweigh the cumulative weight of the bear's factual case.

My conclusion: the risk/reward is unfavorable at current levels. The bull's thesis relies on multiple "ifs" breaking the right way; the bear's thesis relies on "what has already happened." In investment debates, the side anchored in demonstrated facts over unproven hopes deserves the benefit of the doubt.

**Strategic Actions**: 1. **Reduce position size.** Current holders should trim exposure by 25-50%, selling into any strength toward the 28-29 yuan resistance zone. Do not wait for a breakout that may never come — the descending trendline of lower highs since January is the dominant technical feature.

2. **Set a hard stop.** If the stock breaks below 25.80 yuan (the April secondary low), exit the remaining position. That break would invalidate the bull's W-bottom thesis and open the door to a retest of the 22.42 yuan low or worse.

3. **Re-entry conditions (do not act yet):** The bull case becomes investable again only when (a) the reduction overhang is fully cleared — watch for completion filings from both shareholders, (b) the company provides a concrete update on the 2025 license-out deal showing progression to a clinical milestone, and (c) the stock breaks above 30 yuan on volume exceeding 50万股, confirming a genuine trend reversal. Until at least two of these three conditions are met, capital is better deployed elsewhere.

4. **For traders considering the bear side:** A modest short or put position is defensible with a stop-loss above 30 yuan, targeting a move back toward the 22-24 yuan range over a 1-3 month horizon as the reduction supply continues to cap rallies. Size this conservatively — the unique DEL asset means short-squeeze risk is real if any positive catalyst emerges.
