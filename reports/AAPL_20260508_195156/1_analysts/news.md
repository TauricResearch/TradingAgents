## 1. Headline: Apple (AAPL) at a Crossroads — AI Catalyst & Intel Deal Reshape the Narrative

### 🔑 Key AAPL Developments This Week

**Apple-Intel Chip Manufacturing Deal (Breakout Story)**
- Apple and Intel reached a **preliminary agreement** for Intel to manufacture some of the chips powering Apple devices (WSJ, Reuters, May 8).
- Intel stock surged **+14–15%** intraday on the news; Apple shares gained **~2%**.
- The Trump administration reportedly **pushed for the deal**, adding a geopolitical tailwind.
- This is a potential **major win for Intel**, which has been struggling with a **33% revenue drop** and is trying to revitalize its manufacturing business.

**Wedbush Upgrades AAPL Price Target to $400**
- Analyst **Dan Ives** raised the target from $350 → **$400**, maintaining an **Outperform** rating.
- Thesis: Apple is a **"sleeping tech giant"** about to see a **major inflection point** in growth.
- Ives estimates **~20% of the world's population** will access AI through an Apple device in the coming years.
- The catalyst is expected to be **iOS 27 / WWDC 2026**, where Apple will unveil its AI strategy to bring **2.5 billion users** into the AI economy.
- **Services revenue** is identified as the main prize.

**Smart Glasses — Apple Playing Catch-Up**
- The smart glasses category is projected to grow from **6M units (2025) → 20M units (2026)**.
- Apple's flagship smart glasses are **not shipping until Q2 2027**, putting them behind Meta and Google in defining the category's inflection.

**AAPL as a "Pick-and-Shovel" AI Play**
- Motley Fool argues Apple could benefit from the AI race by selling **AI-friendly laptops** (MacBook Air/Pro with M-series chips optimized for local AI workloads).
