## 2. 🤖 Autonomous Vehicles & Robotaxi: Progress but Not Priced In

The company confirmed **unsupervised rides** in the Austin coverage zone, marking a significant milestone in its Full Self-Driving (FSD) commercialization journey. This was announced via Tesla's official robotaxi X account and confirmed by multiple outlets including GuruFocus and Just Auto.

- **Implication:** This is genuine operational progress that validates Tesla's Robotaxi thesis. However, *Barron's* noted that the autonomous vehicles are "finally driving themselves — but not the stock," suggesting markets expected more. The fact that the stock *fell* on the day of broader market gains points to mixed investor reaction.

### Competitive FSD Landscape
- **Waymo** continues to expand its own robotaxi infrastructure, with a new deal to repurpose spent robotaxi batteries as grid storage (via B2U Storage Solutions) — a sign of Waymo's operational maturity.
- **BYD** is directly challenging Tesla in China with an **accident-liability warranty on its Urban NOA (Navigate on Autopilot)** system, a bold move that raises the competitive bar on ADAS accountability and could pressure Tesla's narrative of superior autonomy. This is a notable threat in China, Tesla's second-largest market.

## 3. 🦾 Optimus Robot & OpenAI Competitive Threat

A significant new concern emerged this week: **OpenAI has announced its return to the robotics business**, directly challenging Tesla's Optimus humanoid robot program. Barron's Associate Editor Al Root highlighted the dynamics of this competition. OpenAI's entry into robotics is notable because:

1. It brings world-class AI/LLM capabilities to physical robots.
2. OpenAI has deep capital resources and partnerships (Microsoft, etc.).
3. It creates a credibility challenge for Optimus, which is already competing with Agility Robotics, Boston Dynamics, and Figure AI.

- **Implication:** Tesla's Optimus premium in its valuation could face compression if OpenAI rapidly demonstrates viable robotic AI. This is a medium-term risk factor.
