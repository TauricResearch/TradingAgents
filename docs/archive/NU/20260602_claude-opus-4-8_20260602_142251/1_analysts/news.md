### 2.1 CFO Transition — The Central Catalyst
The single biggest driver of `NU`'s recent weakness is the **departure of longtime CFO Guilherme Lago**, effective July 13, 2026. His replacement, **Rob Livingston**, brings credentials from Visa and Capital One — strong names in global payments and credit — which the company is framing as a signal of its U.S. market expansion ambitions.

**Bear Case (BofA's View):**
- BofA Securities **downgraded `NU` to "Underperform"** and slashed its price target dramatically from **$16 → $10**, citing Lago's exit as part of a **string of senior leadership departures** the broker says are becoming "harder to ignore."
- Leadership instability at the C-suite level is a red flag for execution risk, particularly as the company enters a critical and complex new market (the U.S.).
- BofA's $10 target implies roughly **24% additional downside** from recent $13.13 trading levels.

**Bull Case (Multiple Analysts):**
- Rob Livingston's Visa/Capital One pedigree is a **strategic hire** that meaningfully accelerates the U.S. banking expansion playbook.
