But the bear and neutral analysts won the arguments that matter for new capital at this price. First, the bull's own probability-weighted EV model yields $314–$326 against a $334 price—the stock is fairly-to-slightly-overvalued even under the optimistic reframe, leaving essentially no buffer. The aggressive analyst's attempt to dismiss this model as 'stale inputs' was correctly identified by the neutral analyst as reverse-engineering a justification for the current price, not genuine model updating. Second, the valuation reframe is decisive: 43x forward earnings is underwritten by ~23% revenue growth, but that growth rate is precisely what decelerates as hyperscaler capex growth compresses from 58% toward 8–20%. A moderate normalization to 12–15% growth implies multiple compression to 28–32x and a $214–$238 stock—a ~29% drawdown in a non-catastrophic scenario on a 1.82-beta name with a 52-week low of $83 reached within the last twelve months. Third, the probability arithmetic is the crux: the aggressive analyst's positive expected value rested on an undefended 10% bear-case probability, which both the conservative and neutral analysts dismantled. Given this business's documented cyclicality and base rates, a 20–25% bear-case probability is more honest, which makes expected value from $334 modestly negative to flat—the precise mathematical justification for holding without adding. Additional bear evidence carries weight: ~1% TTM FCF yield (structurally absent margin of safety, making the valuation entirely dependent on the growth timeline materializing), Q3 operating cash conversion deterioration, technical extremity (RSI 71, ~25% above the 50-day SMA, riding the upper Bollinger band), and the under-discussed export-control tail risk that bypasses all leading-indicator monitoring and can only be managed via position sizing. The reason this is not a Sell: the bear never produced confirmation the cycle is turning—only that precursors are flickering—and you don't dump a mid-cycle compounder on unconfirmed signals. The reason it is not a Buy: you don't chase a quality name above its own fair-value model into decelerating growth with no margin of safety. Hold what you have, tighten risk discipline, and let the leading indicators—book-to-bill below 1.0, memory utilization below ~80%, sequential down-revisions to hyperscaler capex guidance, HBM4 order slippage, further cash-conversion deterioration, and China export-control escalation—dictate the next move.
