The bull's own probability-weighted expected return came out at +1% to +5.75% — and against a 4%+ risk-free rate, that's a thin risk-adjusted premium for taking on peak-cycle semiconductor risk.

The cycle "turning" is asserted as imminent but not demonstrated — every current data point (revenue, margins, guidance, design wins) points UP, not down. Selling outright bets against a confirmed, accelerating uptrend with no evidence the inflection has begun. That's a strong claim the bear couldn't fully substantiate.

And why Hold rather than Buy or Overweight? Because the bear successfully dismantled the "valuation is actually cheap" pillar of the bull case. Paying ~29x forward on peak-margin, above-peak-cycle consensus EPS, into expanding DSO and a stock that hasn't made a new high in three weeks, is NOT a high-conviction entry for fresh capital. The margin of safety is thin to absent.

This is the textbook situation the rating scale reserves Hold for: genuinely balanced evidence where the quality argues against selling but the valuation and late-cycle signals argue against adding. The single cleanest resolution — Q3 earnings, specifically gross margin and Q4 guidance, not just headline revenue — is a known, near-term catalyst. The disciplined move is to hold existing exposure and let that print arbitrate before committing fresh capital.
