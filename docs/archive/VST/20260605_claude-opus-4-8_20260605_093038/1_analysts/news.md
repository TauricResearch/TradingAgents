A notable **headwind for the broader utilities sector** was flagged this week: the 10-year Treasury yield sits at **4.45%, near its 91st percentile of trailing 12-month range**. At this yield level, traditional "bond proxy" utilities face direct competition from risk-free Treasuries. However, this risk is **most acute for regulated, dividend-focused utilities** — not for growth-oriented IPPs like VST, which derives its investment thesis from power price leverage and contracted AI/data center growth rather than yield.

The **PJM grid's March 2027 decision on a Data Center Framework** is becoming a critical regulatory catalyst to watch. PJM manages the largest competitive electricity market in North America, and how it structures data center interconnection and capacity market rules will materially impact VST's revenue from its Mid-Atlantic and Midwest fleet. This is a **medium-term regulatory risk and opportunity**.

On balance, for a diversified generator with long-term contracts, higher power prices are typically net positive.
