## 1. 🏢 Company Overview & Recent Developments

Vistra Corp. (NYSE: **VST**) is one of the largest competitive power generators in the United States, operating a diversified fleet spanning **natural gas, nuclear, coal, solar, and battery energy storage**. ## 2. 🔑 Key VST-Specific News (May 28 – June 4, 2026)

### ✅ Cogentrix Acquisition Completed
Vistra has **completed its acquisition of Cogentrix natural gas assets**, significantly expanding its power generation footprint. This move strengthens VST's natural gas capacity at a moment when AI-driven data center demand is creating a "renaissance" for gas power infrastructure. The deal positions Vistra to capture premium power prices in tight grid markets.

### ✅ Nuclear Expansion: Perry Plant Restart + Meta Deal
Vistra is actively working to **restart the Perry nuclear plant**, adding critical baseload capacity. Simultaneously, the company has secured a **new long-term nuclear power agreement with Meta's data centers** — a landmark deal that underscores hyperscaler appetite for 24/7 clean baseload power. Nuclear assets are increasingly valued for their ability to provide firm, carbon-free electricity to AI infrastructure.

### ✅ Analyst Price Target Raised
At least one Wall Street analyst has **raised VST's price target by $4**, citing the company's strategic positioning in AI power demand, nuclear assets, and its expanding contracted revenue base. VST is also ranked **#4 in the "Claude AI Stock Portfolio"** (Insider Monkey/hedge fund tracking), with **102 hedge funds** holding the stock as of the latest filings.

### ✅ AI Energy Stock Recognition
Multiple sources rank VST among the **best AI energy stocks to buy**, driven by its unique combination of nuclear (firm, 24/7 clean power), natural gas (flexible peaker capacity), and now expanded gas assets via Cogentrix. The Vistra vs. Talen Energy comparison piece highlights VST's clean-power fleet as a key differentiator for winning AI data center power purchase agreements (PPAs).

### ⚠️ Valuation & Share Price Pullback
The stock has experienced a **short-term pullback** (down 3.4% in one session), prompting valuation scrutiny. While long-term returns remain exceptional, near-term pressure from rate dynamics and sector rotation creates uncertainty. The stock's YTD and quarterly performance has softened.

### ⚠️ Insider Selling — Bearish Signal
A Simply Wall St. report flags **significant insider stock disposals** over the past year. While not necessarily a definitive sell signal, persistent insider selling warrants monitoring as it may reflect management's view on near-term valuation ceilings.

### ⚠️ Revenue Comparison vs. Constellation Energy
A Motley Fool analysis comparing Vistra and Constellation Energy noted **"dramatic revenue swings"** at Vistra versus Constellation's more stable performance. This highlights VST's exposure to merchant power price volatility in deregulated markets (notably Texas/ERCOT).

## 3. 🌍 Macroeconomic & Sector Context

### ⚡ AI Power Demand — Structural Tailwind
Bloomberg/KKR analysis confirms a **surge in electricity demand from AI and data centers is reshaping North American power markets**, creating a multi-year structural tailwind for independent power producers like VST. KKR partner Brandon Freiman called this a "renaissance" for natural gas after decades of limited new builds. This is the core macro thesis supporting VST's premium valuation.

### 📈 Gas Turbine Supply Crunch
GE Vernova analysis highlights a **global gas turbine supply crunch**, with utilities booking capacity years in advance. This creates a competitive moat for VST's existing and newly acquired natural gas assets — new entrants face 3–5 year lead times just to procure equipment.

### 📉 Utilities as "Bond Proxies" Under Pressure
A critical 24/7 Wall St. piece warns that **the 10-year Treasury yield at 4.45% (91st percentile of its trailing 12-month range)** is breaking the traditional "bond proxy" case for defensive utility stocks. High risk-free rates directly compete with utility dividends, pressuring regulated utility valuations. **However, VST is a competitive/merchant power generator, NOT a regulated utility**, meaning its growth story is more tied to power market economics than dividend yield compression.

### 🏗️ PJM Data Center Framework — Key Watch Item
The **PJM March 2027 Data Center Framework Decision** is flagged as a critical regulatory catalyst for the sector. VST has significant exposure to PJM grid markets. A favorable framework could unlock additional data center power deals; an adverse ruling could cap load growth timelines.

### 💰 Macro Headwinds: Inflation, Iran War, Consumer Stress
Global macro context shows **rising inflationary pressures** (oil prices higher due to Iran conflict, consumer goods price hikes at Walmart), which could influence Fed rate decisions. Persistently high rates are the primary headwind for capital-intensive utilities and power generators. VST's debt load from the Cogentrix acquisition could face higher refinancing costs if rates stay elevated.
