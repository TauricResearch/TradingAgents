## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Texas Instruments Incorporated (TXN) is one of the world's largest semiconductor companies, headquartered in Dallas, Texas. The company designs, manufactures, and sells analog and embedded processing chips used across industrial, automotive, personal electronics, communications, and enterprise markets. TXN operates two primary business segments:

- **Analog Semiconductors** — Power management, signal chain, and high-volume analog products
- **Embedded Processing** — Microcontrollers, DSPs, and processors

TXN differentiates itself through its vertically integrated manufacturing (owning its own fabs), a massive 100,000+ product portfolio, direct customer relationships, and one of the most consistent dividend growth histories in the semiconductor industry. As of June 2026, TXN holds a **market capitalization of approximately $280.4 billion**, making it one of the most valuable semiconductor companies globally.

## 7. ⚙️ Strategic & Business Analysis

### CapEx Investment Cycle — The Key Thesis
TXN has embarked on one of the largest capital investment programs in its history, building multiple new 300mm wafer fabs under its **"Competitive Advantage through Manufacturing" strategy** (aligned with the U.S. CHIPS and Science Act):
- **RFAB2** (Richardson, TX) — Online
- **LFAB** (Lehi, Utah, former Micron facility) — Ramping
- **Sherman, TX fabs** — Under construction (300mm)

Gross PPE has risen from **$9.95B (2022) to $17.87B (Q1 2026)** — an increase of ~$7.9B in four years. This investment is designed to dramatically lower TXN's manufacturing costs and provide significant supply chain advantages over the long term.

**CapEx normalization is the key catalyst:** As fab construction winds down through 2026–2027, CapEx is expected to decline from the ~$4.5–5.1B range (2023–2024) back toward $2–3B, which would unlock significant free cash flow. The sequential decline from $1.305B in Q2 2025 to $676M in Q1 2026 is an early signal that this normalization has begun.

### Inventory Normalization
Inventory peaked at $4.83B in Q3 2025 and is gradually declining ($4.70B in Q1 2026). The semiconductor industry-wide inventory correction that weighed on 2023–2024 revenue appears to be resolving, and TXN's revenue recovery confirms improving end-market demand.

### Dividend Sustainability
The ~$5B/year dividend program is a cornerstone of TXN's capital return story. While currently exceeding FCF, the company has an explicit long-term target to return **all free cash flow to shareholders** via dividends and buybacks. As CapEx declines and revenue grows, dividend coverage will improve substantially.

## 8. ⚠️ Key Risks

1. **Debt Levels:** Total debt of $14.1B and net debt of $10.5B are at multi-year highs. Rising interest rates could increase refinancing costs (interest expense ran at $141M/quarter in recent periods).
2. **Dividend Coverage Gap:** Annual dividends (~$5B) still exceed annual FCF (~$2.6B), requiring continued debt funding in the near term.
3. **Valuation Premium:** At 52.6x trailing P/E and 32.7x forward P/E, there is limited margin of safety if earnings recovery is slower than expected.
4. **Geopolitical Risks:** While TXN is domestically focused, it sells into global markets including China (historically a significant revenue contributor), which faces ongoing trade tensions.
5. **Cyclical Nature:** Semiconductor demand is inherently cyclical; any economic slowdown could delay the anticipated revenue recovery.
6. **Execution Risk on Fab Ramp:** New fabs must be ramped efficiently to generate the projected cost advantages; delays or yield issues would weigh on margins.
