## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

Texas Instruments Incorporated (TXN) is one of the world's largest semiconductor companies, headquartered in Dallas, Texas. TXN designs and manufactures analog chips and embedded processors primarily for industrial and automotive end markets. The company's two primary business segments are **Analog** (the vast majority of revenue) and **Embedded Processing**, with a small "Other" segment. TXN is renowned for its vertically integrated, proprietary-fab model, a massive 300mm wafer manufacturing program, and a shareholder-return culture built on consistent dividend growth and buybacks. TXN's analog chips are used in virtually every electronic system, making it a bellwether for the broader semiconductor cycle.

## 6. 🏗️ Strategic & Operational Analysis

### 300mm Fab Investment Program
TXN's defining strategy is the aggressive build-out of 300mm wafer fabrication capacity. 300mm wafers produce ~2.3x more chips than 200mm wafers at ~$0.10 per chip lower cost. Gross PPE has gone from $9.95B (FY2022) to $17.87B (Q1 2026) — a $7.9B investment in 3.5 years. The drag on margins and FCF has been significant, but **the capex cycle is clearly decelerating** (Q1 2026 capex of $676M vs. peak quarters above $1.2B), meaning the "investment hangover" phase is concluding.

### Industrial & Automotive End Markets
TXN's revenue mix is heavily weighted to **industrial** (~40% historically) and **automotive** (~25%), both of which are long-cycle, high-reliability, sticky markets. The industrial recovery has been gradual but is showing in sequential revenue improvement. Automotive content per vehicle continues to grow structurally.

### Inventory Dynamics
Inventory of ~$4.7B remains elevated versus pre-investment cycle norms ($2.8B in FY2022). This reflects deliberate channel build-up and strategic safety stock. The modest sequential decline in Q1 2026 ($4.70B vs. $4.80B) could indicate the early stages of inventory normalization/drawdown, which is a positive signal.

### Dividend Growth & Shareholder Returns
TXN has raised its quarterly dividend consistently (from ~$1.15/share in early 2022 to ~$1.36/share in 2025 on a quarterly basis). Annual dividend outflows have grown from $4.30B (FY2022) → $5.00B (FY2025). The payout is comfortably covered as FCF recovers.

4. **Margin Recovery Pace:** Gross margins at ~58% are well below the FY2022 peak of ~69%. The pace of margin recovery depends on utilization ramp at new fabs — delays could disappoint.
5. **Inventory Overhang:** If end demand softens, the elevated $4.7B inventory could necessitate write-downs or prolonged destocking.
6. **Competition:** Intensifying competition from STMicroelectronics, Renesas, Infineon, NXP and Chinese analog competitors.
