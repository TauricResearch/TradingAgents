Net: the bull wins the business-quality debate decisively, but the bear wins the price-and-entry debate. When a company this good is trading at a stretched multiple where even generous forward math produces roughly market-level returns, and both analysts agree a better entry exists below current levels, the honest call isn't a high-conviction Buy and it isn't a Sell on a compounder of this caliber — it's Hold. Maintain exposure to a wonderful business, but don't chase it 19.6% above the 200-day into an RSI-overbought, catalyst-uncertain (UBS WWDC caution, SpaceX IPO liquidity) setup at the top of its valuation range.
