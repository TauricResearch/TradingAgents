The revenue inflection the entire bull thesis depends on is perpetually "beginning" but not yet in the data. The bull even conceded a falsifiability test (20%+ cloud growth by Q2 FY2027) — meaning by his own framing, the thesis is currently unproven.

5. Technicals confirm a downtrend, not a reversal: price below 10-EMA and 50-SMA, declining 50-SMA, negative MACD, RSI at 43.86 (NOT oversold). The "third test of support" is statistically weaker than prior tests, and the mainland-investor ADR restriction is a genuine structural reduction in the buyer pool that won't reverse on diplomatic goodwill — it's tied to China's capital controls.

So why Hold rather than Sell or Buy? The valuation floor is real enough that I can't endorse a Sell — at 13.64x forward with a genuine AI optionality and a dominant China commerce/cloud franchise, shorting or fully exiting risks getting caught in exactly the violent recovery the bull describes. But the Buy case requires too many things to break right simultaneously (revenue inflection, margin recovery, regulatory calm, portfolio mean-reversion, buyer-pool restoration), the cash generation is actively deteriorating, the trend is down, and the catalyst (cloud/AI acceleration) is not yet in the numbers. That's the definition of a genuinely balanced setup where the next earnings print resolves the core question. We wait for evidence rather than front-running an unproven inflection.
