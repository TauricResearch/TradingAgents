The bear has the better grasp of the near-term reality — negative FCF, accelerating capex, margin compression, and a confirmed technical downtrend (death cross, price below all major MAs, gap-ups sold into). The bull has the better grasp of the long-term optionality and the fact that a great deal of pessimism is already in the price. Critically, the bear's own honest valuation work puts fair value within ~8% of the current price, which is not the asymmetry you need to justify a Sell, and the bull's own work concedes you need to assume a lot goes right to justify a Buy. When both sides' best-faith fair-value estimates cluster around the current price, that is the textbook definition of a balanced, low-conviction situation — which is why I'm landing on Hold rather than forcing a directional call the evidence doesn't support. The decisive swing factor will be the next one or two earnings prints: any sign of FCF inflection, SG&A moderation, or cloud-driven revenue acceleration tips this toward the bull; continued FCF destruction with elevated SG&A tips it toward the bear.
