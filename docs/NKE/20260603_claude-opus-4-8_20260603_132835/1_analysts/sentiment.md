### 3. Dominant Narrative Themes

1. **Turnaround skepticism:** The central debate is whether NKE's "Win Now" strategy, inventory discipline, and wholesale resets will actually translate into earnings recovery. No definitive evidence either way yet.
2. **Technical vulnerability:** Multiple sources flag sub-50-day SMA, head-and-shoulders, and double-top formations. The stock's inability to hold above $48 is a repeated concern.
3. **China risk:** Weak China sales remain an overhang — both from demand-side (consumer weakness) and supply-side (Vietnam tariff probes threatening manufacturing cost base).
4. **Brand vs. execution gap:** The 95% brand affinity stat is remarkable, but the market is penalizing execution failures, not the brand. This gap is the core bull/bear fulcrum.
5. **Q4 Earnings catalyst (due ~June 30):** Multiple sources frame the upcoming earnings as a make-or-break moment for the turnaround narrative. This creates binary near-term risk.
6. **Management credibility:** CEO and management criticism appears across StockTwits and implicitly in news (AI illusion commentary from COO, margin questions). Leadership trust deficit is a headwind.
