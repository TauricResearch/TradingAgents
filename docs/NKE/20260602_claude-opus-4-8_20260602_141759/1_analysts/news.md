### 1.2 Turnaround Thesis: "Win Now" Strategy
Nike is executing a strategic pivot branded the **"Win Now" initiative**, focused on:
- **Inventory discipline**: Reducing bloated SKU counts and trimming excess inventory that weighed on margins in prior quarters.
- **Innovation focus**: Re-centering R&D around performance footwear to recapture athletic credibility lost to rivals like On Running, HOKA, and New Balance.
- **Wholesale reset**: Rebalancing the DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) vs. wholesale channel mix, reversing the over-reliance on DTC that alienated key retail partners.

Zacks notes these levers "may be building momentum," though the turnaround is not yet confirmed in earnings. Critically, NKE's most recent quarterly results **beat analyst expectations**, lending some credibility to the recovery narrative. Management also outlined plans to **shift manufacturing away from China** to mitigate tariff exposure — a strategically important move given the escalating trade environment.

- **Vietnam Trade Probe**: The U.S. has launched a **third trade investigation into Vietnam** targeting intellectual property rights violations, potentially paving the way for new tariffs. This is directly relevant to NKE: Vietnam is Nike's single largest manufacturing country (~50% of footwear sourced there). New tariffs could dramatically increase COGS and compress margins.
- **China Tariff Shift**: Nike has already begun shifting production away from China, which management explicitly cited on their recent earnings call. However, the Vietnam escalation suggests the "move to Vietnam" hedge may itself face new levies.
- **Tariff-Driven Inflation**: Commentary from the 24/7 Wall St./Rich Habits Podcast notes tariff-driven inflation is becoming "more entrenched," with "higher for longer" as the base case for rates. This constrains the Fed's ability to provide relief and impacts discretionary consumer spending broadly.
