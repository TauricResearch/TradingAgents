**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: Alright, let me walk you through where I landed after weighing both sides of this AMAT debate, because it was a genuinely strong exchange on both ends.

The bull's core case is hard to argue with on the fundamentals: record Q2 FY2026 revenue of $7.91B (+11.4% YoY), record EPS of $3.51 (+33.5% YoY), gross margins near 50%, ROE of ~40%, a near-net-cash balance sheet ($8.24B cash vs $7.27B debt), $6.28B returned to shareholders in FY2025, and a contracted order book extending past 2027. The "picks-and-shovels" framing is real — AMAT wins regardless of which chip architecture dominates because every leading-edge fab needs its deposition, etch, and CMP tools. Technicals are constructively aligned (RSI 64, MACD histogram expanding positive, price above all major moving averages), institutional targets sit at $500-540, and the AI capex supercycle has genuine, monetizing demand behind it (Azure, Google Cloud, Meta), not just speculative anticipation. The bull also did real work answering the hard questions — the $1.4B receivables build is mechanically consistent with record late-quarter shipments to blue-chip counterparties (TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix), and the impairments coexisting with record operating income ($2.52B) strongly suggest they are technology-transition write-downs rather than operational deterioration.

But the bear landed several blows that I cannot dismiss, and they are what keep me from a full Buy. First, the valuation is genuinely demanding: 45x trailing, 29.7x forward on a $16.17 estimate that requires ~52% EPS growth, and 16x book. That leaves little cushion for disappointment, and semiconductor equipment is structurally cyclical — even the bull explicitly conceded the cycle will turn and the stock will decline when it does. Second, there are three legitimately unresolved items the bull himself conceded: the $1.138B in impairment charges across two quarters lack specific explanation, Q2 FCF of $210M is unvalidated until Q3 collection proves out, and current China revenue exposure is genuinely unquantified in the available data. Third, with a 1.654 beta after a ~180% run and crowded bullish sentiment (86% retail bulls, 8.2/10 score), the downside is amplified and the marginal-buyer pool is thinner than at lower prices.

Where I come down: the weight of evidence — record fundamentals, contracted multi-year backlog, fortress balance sheet, structural AI positioning, and aligned technicals — favors the bull thesis enough to warrant constructive exposure. The bear's strongest points are real risks and valid monitoring items, but they are largely "wait for a better price / prove it out" arguments rather than evidence of a broken thesis. That argues against a Hold (the evidence is not balanced — the bull has the stronger structural case) but also against a full Buy at all-time highs with three open questions and stretched multiples. Overweight captures the right posture: lean into the strength, but size and pace entries with discipline rather than chasing at the highs.
