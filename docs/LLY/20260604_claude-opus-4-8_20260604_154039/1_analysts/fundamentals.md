## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

Eli Lilly and Company (`LLY`) is one of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies, headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. With roots dating back to 1876, Lilly has evolved into a global biopharmaceutical leader with a diversified portfolio spanning diabetes/obesity (GLP-1 drugs), oncology, immunology, and neuroscience. The company's flagship products — **Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide)** and **Verzenio (abemaciclib)** — have driven an extraordinary multi-year revenue surge. Lilly operates in over 100 countries, employing approximately 45,000+ people globally.

## 7. ⚠️ Risks & Considerations

1. **Valuation Risk:** At ~40x trailing earnings and ~1 trillion market cap, any slowdown in GLP-1 demand or competitive pricing pressure could trigger sharp devaluations.
2. **Competition:** Novo Nordisk's semaglutide products (Ozempic/Wegovy) remain formidable competitors. New entrants (oral GLP-1s, Amgen, Pfizer) could disrupt market dynamics.
3. **Pricing & Drug Pricing Policy Risk:** The Inflation Reduction Act and ongoing pharmaceutical pricing reforms remain headwinds to long-term revenue.
4. **Unusual Charges:** LLY has recorded significant one-time charges in every quarter (Q1 2025: -$1.61B, Q3 2025: -$0.97B, Q1 2026: -$0.86B), primarily restructuring/M&A related, which suppress reported GAAP earnings.
5. **Debt Financing Risk:** With $43B+ in debt and continuing heavy CapEx commitments, rising interest rates or credit market disruptions could increase borrowing costs.
6. **Inventory Concentration Risk:** $14.5B in inventory with $8.5B in WIP is elevated; supply chain disruptions could impair cash flow.
7. **Tax Rate:** Effective tax rate has risen from ~8.3% (2022) to ~19.8% (2025 annual), which could be a headwind if global minimum tax measures further increase the rate.
