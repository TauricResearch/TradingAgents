## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

Eli Lilly and Company (`LLY`) is one of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies, headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. The company discovers, develops, manufactures, and markets pharmaceutical products globally. Its portfolio is centered around diabetes and obesity treatments (notably GLP-1 receptor agonists **tirzepatide** – marketed as Mounjaro® for diabetes and Zepbound® for obesity), oncology, immunology, and neuroscience. Lilly has established itself as the world's largest publicly traded healthcare company by market capitalization.

## 9. ⚠️ Risk Factors

1. **Valuation Risk:** At $1T+ market cap with a 41x P/E, any slowdown in GLP-1 adoption or pricing pressure could cause significant multiple compression.
2. **Competitive Risk:** Novo Nordisk (semaglutide), emerging GLP-1 competitors (AstraZeneca, Pfizer, etc.) may erode LLY's market share over time.
3. **Concentration Risk:** Significant portion of revenue tied to tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound). Patent cliff or safety concerns could be catastrophic.
4. **Manufacturing Risk:** Massive CapEx and WIP inventory ($8.46B) suggest LLY is betting heavily on continued demand; any demand shortfall would impact margins.
5. **Drug Pricing/Policy Risk:** U.S. Medicare drug price negotiation (IRA) and global pricing pressures could cap revenue growth.
6. **Leverage:** $43.4B total debt with $38.1B net debt is substantial, though manageable given $16.8B+ annual operating cash flows.
7. **Unusual Charges:** Recurring "special charges" (~$3.4B in 2025, $863M in Q1 2026) include acquisitions, write-offs, and restructuring costs.
