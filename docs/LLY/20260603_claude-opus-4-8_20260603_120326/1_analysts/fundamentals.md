## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Eli Lilly and Company is one of the world's largest and most iconic pharmaceutical corporations, headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. Founded in 1876, Lilly has evolved into a dominant force in global healthcare, with a market capitalization of approximately **$972 billion** as of June 3, 2026 — making it one of the most valuable companies in the world, let alone among pharmaceutical peers.

Lilly's blockbuster portfolio is currently headlined by **tirzepatide** (Mounjaro for T2 diabetes, Zepbound for obesity), **dulaglutide** (Trulicity), **abemaciclib** (Verzenio for breast cancer), and **LY3002813** (donanemab for Alzheimer's disease). Its GLP-1-driven growth trajectory is arguably the strongest in the pharmaceutical sector, with a clear and compounding revenue growth narrative driving market enthusiasm and pricing.

## 7. 🔍 Key Risks & Considerations

1. **High Leverage**: Debt-to-equity of 139x is elevated. While manageable given cash flows, rising interest rates or unexpected revenue deceleration could pressure coverage ratios.
2. **Patent Cliff / Competitive Risk**: The GLP-1 obesity market is attracting increasing competition (Novo Nordisk, other entrants). Pricing and market share dynamics will be critical to monitor.
3. **CapEx Intensity**: With $10.85B in CapEx in 2025 and $2.53B already in Q1 2026, Lilly is betting enormous capital on demand continuation. Overcapacity is a risk if demand moderates.
4. **Policy/Regulatory Risk**: Drug pricing reform (IRA, CMS negotiations), FDA approval risks for pipeline drugs, and international reimbursement headwinds could impact revenues.
5. **Concentration Risk**: An outsized portion of revenue comes from the GLP-1 franchise (Mounjaro/Zepbound). Any clinical or safety setback would be material.
6. **Valuation**: Though the forward PE has compressed to ~24.5x, any EPS growth miss relative to consensus expectations could cause significant valuation derating given the high absolute market cap.
7. **Inventory Build**: Rapid inventory accumulation ($14.5B) may indicate potential for write-downs if demand forecasts don't materialize.
