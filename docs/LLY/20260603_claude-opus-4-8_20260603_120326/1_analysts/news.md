## 2. COMPANY-SPECIFIC DEVELOPMENTS (LLY)

### 🔬 A. Gene-Editing Breakthrough: $1.9B Ascidian Therapeutics Deal
Eli Lilly signed a **landmark collaboration and licensing agreement worth up to $1.9 billion** with **Ascidian Therapeutics** (reported by both The Wall Street Journal and BioPharma Dive). The deal grants Lilly exclusive rights to Ascidian's **RNA exon-editing technology** — a next-generation approach that **repairs faulty genes without permanently altering DNA**, which is a key regulatory and ethical advantage over traditional CRISPR methods. The deal targets **genetically driven kidney diseases**, a large unmet medical need. Multiple financial and scientific outlets (Motley Fool: "Gene editing could be pharma's next gold rush") view this as highly strategic. This adds another major pillar to Lilly's pipeline beyond its existing GLP-1 dominance.

### 💊 B. GLP-1 Franchise: An Emerging 90-Million Customer Market
Wells Fargo's chief agriculture economist Dr. Michael Swanson stated that **GLP-1 weight-loss drugs will eventually match statins at ~90 million prescriptions** — a market Lilly is at the center of alongside Novo Nordisk. Mounjaro and Zepbound **combined for $12.8 billion in Q1 2026 alone**, a staggering figure that contributed to LLY reporting **55.5% revenue growth in a single quarter**. Full-year 2026 guidance has been raised to **$82B–$85B**. The GLP-1 category is not a trend or fad — as analysts note, it is now a prescription-driven, institutionalized therapeutic class here to stay.

### 🏥 C. ASCO 2026: Oncology Franchise Expanding
At **ASCO 2026**, Eli Lilly's **Retvemo (selpercatinib)** demonstrated **positive data for adjuvant non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)**, expanding its potential indication. This is a meaningful catalyst for the oncology segment and further diversifies LLY's revenue base beyond metabolic disease. Positive trial readouts add pipeline optionality and demonstrate the breadth of Lilly's R&D engine.

### 🔬 D. "Busy Dealmaking Week" — GLP-2 Obesity Drug Acquisition
According to BioPharma Dive, in the same week as the Ascidian deal, Lilly also **acquired rights to a GLP-2 obesity drug** from Hanmi Pharmaceutical. This positions LLY to pursue a combination GLP-1/GLP-2 approach that may offer superior weight-loss efficacy, potentially leapfrogging current standard-of-care treatments and outcompeting rivals.

### 📈 E. Long-Term Valuation Discussion: LLY vs. SpaceX by 2028?
Motley Fool published a bold prediction that **Eli Lilly will be worth more than SpaceX within 2 years**, citing its revenue ramp, pipeline depth, and institutional tailwinds from GLP-1 adoption. A separate analysis asks whether LLY can reach **$1,500/share by 2028** (from current ~$1,082.92). While speculative, these analyses reflect strong Street-level bullishness and support a favorable long-term narrative.

### 🧬 F. Broader Biotech Tailwinds: Longevity & AI-Driven Biology
Major capital allocators — including Jeff Bezos, NVIDIA, and now Coinbase founder Brian Armstrong (through NewLimit, valued at $3.1B after tripling) — are aggressively investing in **AI-driven biology and longevity**. This macro sector trend benefits LLY indirectly, as Lilly's RNA editing and GLP-1 assets align with the longevity investment thesis. Lilly's brand and scientific credibility position it as a prime beneficiary of this capital flow.

### 📉 G. Year-to-Date Underperformance Relative to Fundamentals
Despite the extraordinary fundamental performance (+55.5% Q1 revenue growth, raised guidance), **LLY shares are up only 1.11% YTD**, sitting at **$1,082.92**. This disconnect between fundamentals and price action may reflect broader market rotation (tech/AI outperformance), valuation digestion from prior run-up, or macro-related risk-off sentiment. It creates a potentially attractive **risk/reward entry point** for fundamental investors.
