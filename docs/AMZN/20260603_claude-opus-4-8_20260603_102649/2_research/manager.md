**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: Alright, here's where I land after sitting with both sides of this debate, which was genuinely high quality on both ends.

First, what both analysts actually agreed on matters enormously: AMZN is a world-class business, AWS is a primary beneficiary of the AI infrastructure supercycle (Goldman's $5.3T capex forecast names it explicitly), operating margins are at record highs (13.14%, gross 51.8%), operating cash flow is compounding ~20%+ annually, and the 200-day SMA at $231.83 confirms the long-term uptrend is structurally intact. When both the bull and bear concede the fundamental quality and the durability of the AWS/advertising/Prime franchise, the debate has effectively narrowed to a question of *entry timing and near-term risk*, not thesis validity. That's a crucial tell.

The bull's strongest arguments: (1) The FCF compression is a recognizable, intentional infrastructure-cycle pattern — OCF/CapEx compressed similarly in 2014 before AWS margins drove a recovery, and operating cash flow has tripled in three years. (2) Higher depreciation is non-cash, so the bear's GAAP-EPS worry actually argues for valuing AMZN on cash flow, where it screens more reasonably. (3) The bear's own re-entry criteria (RSI <40 + MACD histogram positive + close above ~$267) embed an awkward problem — the confirmation signal can fire above the exit price, making the market-timing round trip hard to execute profitably. (4) Trying to time a $10–$15 trading band on a generational compounder is precisely the behavior that causes investors to miss the best days.

The bear's strongest arguments — and these are legitimate, which is why I'm not going to a full Buy: (1) The near-term technical setup is genuinely deteriorating — price below the Bollinger Lower Band, 10-EMA, VWMA and Bollinger Middle simultaneously, MACD days from a bearish zero-line cross, RSI at 44 and falling. The 50-SMA at ~$248 is being tested with the worst momentum backdrop since the February lows. (2) The Q1 2026 headline EPS was inflated by ~$15.68B in one-time security gains, so normalized earnings are materially below the reported number, and the forward EPS of $9.86 carries real execution risk once you account for accelerating depreciation. (3) FCF went negative (-$18.17B in Q1) with CapEx ($44.2B/qtr, ~$177B annualized) growing faster than OCF in the most recent quarter — the deficit is widening, not narrowing, right now. (4) The 12.5pp YTD underperformance vs QQQ is a real, sustained five-month signal of institutional rotation toward pure-play AI names, not a one-day artifact. (5) The macro picture (supply-side energy inflation, consumer stress) hits the lowest-margin retail segment hardest precisely over the next couple of quarters.

So how do I adjudicate? The bear essentially won the *near-term timing* argument — there is a credible path to $248 and possibly $231 over the next few weeks, and momentum is not your friend today. But the bear never disproved the thesis; they explicitly conceded it, and even their own re-entry plan presumes buying the same business back at lower-to-similar prices. That is a tactical disagreement, not a structural sell case. A SELL/UNDERWEIGHT rating implies conviction the business or its long-run value is impaired — neither analyst established that.

The bull, meanwhile, didn't fully neutralize the near-term downside risk (the 'just buy now' urgency leaned on FOMO), and the 'Strong Buy / 4:1 with a stop at $246' framing is internally a bit inconsistent — a 4% stop on a stock whose next real support is 10% lower is thin. But the bull is correct that the cumulative weight of confirmed, in-progress catalysts (AWS AI ramp, 20%+ advertising growth, international margin recovery, FCF normalization into 2027) tilts the 12–18 month risk/reward to the upside.

Net: the durable, agreed-upon fundamental strength and asymmetric long-term upside outweigh a real but tactical near-term technical/timing risk. That combination — constructive on the business, but with enough near-term risk that I won't chase at the high end of the range — is the textbook definition of Overweight rather than Buy or Hold. Accumulate with discipline; let the pullback work in your favor rather than betting against the franchise.
