## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

**Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN)** is the world's largest e-commerce retailer and a dominant cloud computing provider. Its diversified business spans:

- **Amazon Web Services (AWS):** The global #1 cloud platform (IaaS/PaaS/SaaS), the primary profit engine
- **North America & International Retail:** First-party and third-party marketplace sales
- **Advertising Services:** One of the fastest-growing digital ad platforms globally
- **Amazon Prime & Subscriptions:** Recurring revenue from ~200M+ members
- **Devices & Alexa:** Echo, Kindle, Fire TV hardware and AI assistant ecosystem
- **AI/ML & Generative AI:** Bedrock, CodeWhisperer, Trainium, Inferentia chip development
- **Logistics:** Amazon's own end-to-end fulfillment and last-mile delivery network

The company trades on NASDAQ under ticker `AMZN` and has a **market capitalization of approximately $2.678 trillion** as of June 3, 2026.

Operating income grew from $12.25B (2022) to **$79.98B (2025)** — on revenue that grew only ~40% over the same period. This extraordinary operating leverage is structural, driven by:
- AWS (cloud infrastructure) scaling at ~60–70%+ margins
- Advertising revenues (~$50B+ annually) at very high margins
- Prime subscription lock-in
- Logistics network efficiencies

### 📦 Theme 4: Balance Sheet Transformation
Amazon has gone from negative working capital and stressed liquidity in 2022 to holding **$143B in cash/investments** in Q1 2026. The equity base has nearly tripled. This fortress balance sheet provides:
- Flexibility for large M&A
- Ability to fund capex without diluting equity
- Competitive moat against smaller cloud rivals

### 🌍 Theme 5: International Expansion & Emerging Markets
International segment revenue continues to recover toward profitability. Margin improvements in EU, India, and latent markets represent a significant untapped earnings pool.

### ⚡ Theme 6: Debt Surge Warrants Monitoring
The Q1 2026 debt issuance of $59.46B was the largest single-quarter capital raise in the company's history. While this is funding productive assets, it has pushed **total debt to $209.89B** and increased leverage ratios. Investors should monitor interest coverage and refinancing risk.
