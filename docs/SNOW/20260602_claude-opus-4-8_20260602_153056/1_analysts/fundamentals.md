## 1. Company Overview

Snowflake Inc. (SNOW) is a cloud-native data platform company that provides data warehousing, data lake, data engineering, data science, and data application development services. ## 7. Key Risks

1. **Persistent GAAP Losses:** SNOW has never posted a GAAP profit. With a cumulative retained earnings deficit of -$10.09B and net losses of ~$1.2B annually, the path to GAAP profitability remains distant. Accelerating SBC ($1.6B/year) is the primary culprit.
2. **Stock-Based Compensation Dilution:** SBC represents ~34% of revenue. While it's non-cash, it dilutes shareholders and masks true economic costs. The adjusted profitability picture is meaningfully better, but management must demonstrate SBC discipline.
3. **Elevated Valuation:** At ~100x forward earnings, SNOW is priced for perfection. Any revenue growth deceleration or macro headwinds could trigger severe multiple compression, as seen in the 52-week range collapse from $284.99 to $118.30.
4. **Leverage Shift:** The $2.3B debt raise fundamentally changes SNOW's balance sheet risk profile. Debt/equity of 143% is high for a growth software company, especially one burning GAAP cash.
5. **Thinning Current Ratio:** Current ratio of 1.053x is low given the working capital needs and suggests limited short-term liquidity buffer outside of investment liquidation.
6. **Competitive Intensity:** SNOW competes directly with cloud giants (AWS Redshift, Google BigQuery, Azure Synapse) that have virtually unlimited resources and can bundle services at aggressive price points.
7. **Acquisition Integration Risk:** Rapid goodwill accumulation ($1.54B, including $343M in one quarter) raises integration and impairment risk if acquired businesses underperform.
8. **Consumption-Based Model Volatility:** Revenue can fluctuate with client usage levels, making forecasting difficult and exposing SNOW to enterprise budget-cutting cycles.
