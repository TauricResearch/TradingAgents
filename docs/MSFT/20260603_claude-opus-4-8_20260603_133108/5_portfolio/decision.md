**Investment Thesis**: The fundamental case is largely settled by reported results that no analyst seriously contested: 18.3% YoY revenue growth, ~$38.4B operating income (up ~20% YoY), 46%+ operating margins, $136B operating cash flow, and net debt of only $8.16B against $184B EBITDA — all at ~22x forward earnings, the lowest multiple in the Mag-7. Critically, Microsoft is funding the largest infrastructure buildout in its history from internal cash flow without dilution, while Alphabet raised $85B in equity. That tilts risk/reward favorably and rules out Hold or lower.

However, three genuine constraints argue against max-conviction Buy. First, the technical posture is unresolved: the stock sits below its 200-DMA ($457.76) and the June 1-2 rejection ($460.52 to $441.31, a $19.21 single-session decline) was not fully explained away. The aggressive analyst's Bollinger mean-reversion rebuttal, followed to its conclusion, actually concedes the stock is in a contested range between support ($430-$445) and resistance (200-DMA) — an environment that rewards staged accumulation, not front-loading. Second, the depreciation-timing asymmetry surfaced by the conservative and neutral analysts is the most underappreciated risk: net PPE grew $77.8B in nine months on assets with 15-20 year lives, so FY2027 depreciation will scale above the current ~$8B/quarter even as the revenue model shifts toward variable, consumption-based recognition (deferred revenue declined from $64.6B to $50.9B over three quarters). Depreciation is fixed; utilization revenue is not. Third, total cash demands (~$166B: CapEx + $24B dividends + $18B buybacks) exceed operating cash flow, with the cash balance down $16.4B in nine months — manageable given the fortress balance sheet, but not the frictionless self-funding narrative the bull presented.

On sizing, the decisive point is that the conservative analyst's own probability estimates (55-60% 200-DMA reclaim, 50-55% favorable CapEx-return timing, only 30-35% near-term EU regulatory headwind) produce positive expected value well above a 25-30% initial tranche; their lower recommendation rests on an over-applied capital-protection utility function. Conversely, the contested-range technical evidence and the unresolved depreciation question argue against the full 50-60% front-load the aggressive analyst urged. The neutral analyst's 40-45% initial tranche correctly maps deployment to the actual state of resolution — two of three risk dimensions substantially resolved in the bull direction, one genuinely open — and reserves the largest add for the highest-quality confirmation signal (a weekly 200-DMA close that restores systematic institutional buying). The EU Cloud Act is best treated as a ~30% probability sentiment-driven multiple-compression event rather than structural impairment, since it targets a government-tender channel rather than the core commercial model, materially weaker than the GDPR analogy. Net: lean in and build, but stage the entry and honor the $416 stop.
