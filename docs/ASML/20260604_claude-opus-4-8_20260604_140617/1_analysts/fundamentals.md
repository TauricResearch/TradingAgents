## 1. Company Profile & Overview

**ASML Holding N.V.** is a Dutch multinational and the undisputed global monopolist in photolithography systems — the machines used to manufacture semiconductor chips. The company's headquarters are in Veldhoven, Netherlands. ASML designs, develops, and sells advanced semiconductor manufacturing equipment, software, and services to virtually every leading chip manufacturer in the world, including TSMC, Samsung, and Intel.

ASML's most strategically critical product line is **Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) Lithography**, of which it is the **sole global supplier**. EUV machines are indispensable for fabricating chips at 7nm, 5nm, 3nm, and 2nm process nodes — the bleeding edge of modern semiconductor technology. The company also manufactures Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) systems and is developing next-generation **High-NA EUV** scanners, extending its technological lead for years to come.

## 7. Segment & Strategic Drivers

### 7.1 EUV Monopoly & Competitive Moat
ASML is the **only company in the world** that can manufacture EUV lithography systems. The complexity of EUV production involves over 100,000 components, a global supply chain spanning decades of institutional knowledge, and proprietary laser-plasma light source technology. No credible challenger exists or is likely to emerge within this decade. This creates a structural, durable moat.

### 7.2 High-NA EUV: The Next Frontier
High-Numerical Aperture (High-NA) EUV systems — the TWINSCAN EXE:5000 — are currently being ramped. These systems are priced at approximately **€350M–€400M each** (versus ~€180M for standard EUV), representing a step-change in ASP uplift. Early adopters include Intel and TSMC. This is the primary driver behind the Forward EPS expansion from $30.04 to $48.18.

### 7.3 Deferred Revenue & Backlog
- Current Deferred Revenue of **$16.0B** (Q4 2025) and Non-Current Deferred Revenue of **$3.37B** signal an extremely well-supported near-term revenue base. This backlog provides high revenue visibility for the next 12–18 months.

### 7.4 Geopolitical Risks
ASML remains subject to **Dutch and U.S. export control regulations**, which restrict EUV system exports to China. DUV systems have also come under increasing restrictions. China has historically accounted for 20-30% of ASML revenue — any further tightening of restrictions could meaningfully reduce the addressable market. This is a key downside risk.

### 7.5 Inventory Buildup Monitoring
Inventory has grown steadily from $7.20B (FY2022) to $11.71B (Q1 2026) — an increase of **62.6%**. While much of this reflects legitimate demand build-ahead and system component staging, it warrants monitoring. If demand were to soften or customers defer orders, inventory impairment risk could emerge.
