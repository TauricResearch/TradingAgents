**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: Alright, let me walk you through how I'm reading this one, because both sides argued well and I don't want to pretend the call is obvious.

The bull's core case is genuinely strong and, importantly, the bear conceded most of it: ASML owns a physics-limited EUV monopoly with no credible competitor on any relevant horizon, it posted record FY2025 results (revenue ~$32.67B, FCF ~$11B, net income +26.9%), Q1 2026 EPS grew ~19% YoY, and there's a €36B confirmed backlog plus ~$16B in customer prepayments sitting as deferred revenue. That deferred revenue point is the one the bear never really neutralized — customers have already paid for machines, which is real visibility you rarely get in a cyclical name. When the bear's own closing concedes "this is one of the greatest businesses ever constructed" and "I'm not asking you to short it," the debate effectively collapses into a valuation-and-timing argument rather than a thesis argument. That matters.

Now, the bear did land real punches, and I'm not waving them away. Three of his points survived scrutiny: (1) the gross margin data is genuinely ambiguous — Q1 2026 at 53.0% is below Q1 2025's 54.0%, and the multi-year range of ~51.6%–54.0% does not yet confirm the step-change margin expansion the bull's High-NA thesis requires; (2) the Intel execution dependency for High-NA acceptance is a legitimate, specific risk given Intel's repeated 18A struggles; and (3) DUV export-control escalation is directional, not theoretical, and could shave a few percent off revenue and trigger multiple compression. The bear is also right that this is a high-beta (1.37) cyclical that demonstrably fell ~19% in eight weeks earlier in the year without fundamental deterioration, and that sentiment is crowded.

Where the bear overreached: his probability table was engineered to produce a negative expected value by assigning ~27% combined weight to moderate/full-bear cyclical-collapse scenarios that are calibrated to prior memory-glut downturns, which are categorically different demand drivers than the current AI-logic capex cycle. And his central recommendation — "wait ten weeks for a 10% pullback" — is, as the bull correctly showed, a market-timing bet on short-term price direction with a thin expected edge against a structurally compounding asset. The "negative FCF" alarm was largely explained by deliberate capital returns and the prepaid manufacturing pipeline.

So where do I come out? The bull won the thesis; the bear won the entry-discipline point. That combination doesn't justify a full-conviction Buy at a stock that's run 138% off its lows, sits near its YTD high with decelerating momentum, carries a 2.42 PEG, and has real (if manageable) margin/Intel/DUV risks that could compress a 35x forward multiple on any stumble. But it's too strong a franchise with too much confirmed forward visibility to sit out or trim. That's a textbook Overweight: constructive, build the position, but do it with discipline rather than chasing the full size at the high.

**Strategic Actions**: 1) Establish/build toward a target weight on ASML, but do not deploy full size at current ~$1,628 levels. Begin with roughly 50-60% of intended position now to ensure participation in the structural compounding thesis the bull won, and stage the remainder.

2) Use a scaled-entry / dollar-cost approach for the balance: add tranches on pullbacks toward the 50-SMA (~$1,459) and again near the 200-SMA (~$1,196) if reached. Both levels were touched within the last 12 months, so the bear's pullback scenario is realistic enough to plan around rather than chase past.

3) Set a stop-discipline / risk anchor: treat a sustained break below the 50-SMA (~$1,459) on deteriorating fundamentals as a reassessment trigger, not the scaling level. Given beta of ~1.37, size the position so a 20-30% drawdown is survivable within portfolio risk limits.

4) Monitor the three live risk catalysts the bear legitimately raised: (a) Q2/Q3 2026 gross margin trajectory — need confirmation margins are trending above the 53% range as High-NA scales; (b) Intel 18A/High-NA acceptance timing — watch for pushed acceptances that would slip revenue recognition; (c) Dutch DUV export-control developments. Any two of these breaking negatively should pause further additions.

5) Trim into strength if the stock reaches the $1,995-$2,090 zone without corresponding upward earnings revisions, since that would re-stretch the multiple; conversely, accelerate the staged buys if Q2/Q3 earnings confirm margin expansion and backlog conversion.

6) Position horizon is 12+ months. This is a constructive accumulation, not a trade — the rating reflects high conviction in the franchise tempered by entry-point and execution risk.
