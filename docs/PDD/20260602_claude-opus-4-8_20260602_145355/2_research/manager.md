The bull's strongest, most durable argument is the valuation-versus-cash-generation asymmetry. Where the bull overreached: the Amazon/Apple/Meta analogies all involve U.S.-domiciled entities with full legal ownership and shareholder recourse — categorically different from a Cayman VIE holding contractual rights over Chinese OpCos. The bull never neutralized that legal-structure distinction; they just argued it's "priced in," which is circular. Where the bear overreached: the probability model that produced -7% EV was no more empirically anchored than the bull's +20%, and the bear leaned hard on a -5x doom multiple scenario that requires a cascade PDD's balance sheet is specifically built to absorb.

So when I net it out: the valuation genuinely over-discounts the franchise on a 12-18 month-plus horizon, which argues against Sell. But the near-term setup — incomplete revision cycle, broken technical structure below all three major MAs, a defensive multi-year capex program with no management-guided margin floor, escalating regulatory surface area, and confirmed earnings contraction — argues against stepping in to Buy or add right now. The two strongest theses are pointing in opposite time directions: long-term value versus near-term momentum and earnings deterioration. That's the textbook definition of a genuinely balanced book where the prudent call is to neither chase the falling knife nor exit a quality asset at maximum pessimism. Hold, and let Q2 2026 plus the revision cycle resolve the ambiguity.
