## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) is the world's dominant designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip (SoC) solutions. Originally founded in 1993, NVIDIA has transformed from a gaming GPU supplier into the critical infrastructure backbone of artificial intelligence (AI), data centers, high-performance computing (HPC), autonomous vehicles, and professional visualization.

Its flagship product families — including Hopper (H100/H200), Blackwell (B100/B200/GB200), and the upcoming architectures — are deeply embedded in virtually every major hyperscaler, cloud provider, enterprise AI lab, and sovereign AI initiative globally. NVIDIA's CUDA software ecosystem further entrenches its moat, as the platform has become the de facto standard for GPU-accelerated computing.

**Key Business Segments:**
- **Data Center:** The dominant revenue driver (~88%+ of total revenue), serving hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta), cloud providers, and enterprise AI.
- **Gaming:** A strong secondary segment recovering on the back of GeForce RTX architecture upgrades.
- **Professional Visualization:** Workstation GPUs for creative professionals and enterprise use.
- **Automotive:** Growing rapidly, with DRIVE platform powering autonomous vehicles.
- **OEM & Other:** Cryptocurrency mining residuals and embedded computing.
