## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) is the world's leading designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip (SoC) technologies. Originally focused on PC gaming graphics, the company has evolved into the dominant infrastructure provider for artificial intelligence (AI), data centers, autonomous vehicles, robotics, and high-performance computing. Its flagship product families include the Hopper and Blackwell GPU architectures, CUDA software ecosystem, NVLink interconnects, and the full-stack DGX AI supercomputer platform.

NVIDIA operates through two primary reporting segments:
- **Data Center** (by far the largest and fastest-growing, driven by AI training and inference demand)
- **Gaming, Professional Visualization, Automotive, and OEM**

## 7. ⚠️ Key Risk Factors

1. **Concentration Risk:** Heavy reliance on AI/data center demand; a slowdown in hyperscaler capex could significantly impact revenues.
2. **Export Controls & Geopolitics:** U.S. government restrictions on chip exports to China and other nations pose ongoing revenue and supply chain risks.
3. **Inventory Build Risk:** A $25.8B inventory balance (up 128% YoY) could lead to writedowns if demand slows unexpectedly.
4. **Competition:** AMD, Intel, and custom chip efforts by Google (TPUs), Amazon (Trainium/Inferentia), and Microsoft pose medium-term threats.
5. **Valuation Premium:** At $5.3 trillion market cap, any growth disappointment could trigger sharp multiple compression.
6. **Customer Concentration:** A small number of hyperscaler customers account for a disproportionate share of revenue.
7. **M&A Integration Risk:** ~$14.5B in acquisitions in FY2026 adds goodwill and integration complexity.
8. **Supply Chain:** Reliance on TSMC for leading-edge fabrication creates concentration risk.
