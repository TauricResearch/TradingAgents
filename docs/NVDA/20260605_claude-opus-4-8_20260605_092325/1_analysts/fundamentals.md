## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) is the world's dominant designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip (SoC) products. Originally known for gaming GPUs, NVIDIA has evolved into the central infrastructure provider for the artificial intelligence (AI) and high-performance computing (HPC) revolution. Its core product lines include data center accelerators (H100, H200, Blackwell architecture), GeForce gaming GPUs, professional visualization (Quadro/RTX), automotive DRIVE platforms, and its proprietary CUDA software ecosystem. NVIDIA's competitive moat lies in both its hardware and its deeply entrenched software stack — CUDA — which provides substantial switching costs for AI developers globally.

## 7. ⚠️ Risk Factors

1. **Regulatory/Export Restrictions:** U.S. government restrictions on chip exports to China represent a meaningful top-line risk, given China's historical contribution to NVIDIA's revenue.
2. **Concentration Risk:** Heavy reliance on a small number of hyperscaler customers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) for data center revenue.
3. **Valuation Premium:** Despite the low PEG, absolute P/B of 32x and TTM P/E of 32x could compress on any growth disappointment.
4. **Competition:** AMD, Intel, and custom silicon from hyperscalers (Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium, etc.) are intensifying competition, though NVIDIA's CUDA moat remains formidable.
5. **Inventory Buildup:** Sequential inventory growth (+20%) warrants monitoring for potential demand softening or supply glut risks.
6. **High Beta (2.20):** NVDA is ~2.2x as volatile as the broader market — portfolio risk management is essential.
7. **Supply Chain Dependencies:** TSMC concentration for advanced node manufacturing creates geopolitical tail risk.
