**Investment Thesis**: The debate resolved decisively that this is a timing-and-price question, not a thesis question. The fundamental case is overwhelming and conceded even by the bear: $81.6B quarterly revenue (+85% YoY), 74.9% gross margins at cycle highs, ~$48.6B quarterly FCF, ~$68B net cash, $19B quarterly buybacks, and a forward P/E of 16.4x against ~94% forward EPS growth (PEG ~0.66). The aggressive analyst's strongest, unrebutted point is that the forward multiple is already below the S&P 500 median, structurally limiting discount-rate compression — and his own stress test showed that even a one-hyperscaler capex softening only lifts the forward P/E to ~18.6x on 80% growth, still a sub-1 PEG. The Vera Rubin/HBM4 supply chain is de-risked with all three memory suppliers certified and in production.

However, the conservative and neutral analysts won the near-term execution argument that a full one-third position before a known binary catalyst is suboptimal. Their strongest, unanswered points are: (1) the institutional reality that a 21% drawdown — the aggressive analyst's own stress-scenario fill at ~$172.50 — is "survivable" mathematically but erodes the political capital needed to deploy later tranches; (2) genuine gap risk, since beta 2.20 and an $8 ATR mean a hot CPI can blow through the $202.69 stop and fill materially lower, converting a macro wobble into a forced exit that forfeits the very five-year thesis; (3) the correlated (not independent) nature of the bear's tail scenario, making the aggressive analyst's 1.5-2.5% joint probability an understatement; and (4) the 20.5% sequential inventory build to $25.8B, which is operationally explicable in a product-cycle ramp but remains unconfirmed by demand data until the next earnings report ~6 weeks out.

The neutral analyst's synthesis is the most defensible: the destination is a Buy, but the speed should be disciplined. A one-quarter initial tranche captures the fundamental quality and any benign-CPI gap-up (beta cuts both ways) while keeping pre-catalyst exposure modest. The critical refinement both sides ultimately converged toward is condition-based pre-commitment for the second tranche — deploying into post-CPI weakness only when CPI is at/below consensus or when NVDA holds the Bollinger lower band ($207) or 50 SMA ($202) with two sessions of volume confirmation — which resolves the conservative analyst's valid objection that a mechanical limit order at $205 automates buying into the most adverse information environment. This lands at Overweight rather than Buy: constructive and accumulating, but with explicit discipline around the entry given the completed weekly TD-9, building monthly TD count, active MACD bearish crossover, oil at $102, and the CPI catalyst days away. Invalidation: a sustained weekly close below $202.69 accompanied by gross margin deterioration or quantified capex cuts from two of the four major hyperscalers.
