Let me be honest: this is a legitimate scenario. It's also the *only* scenario that makes the bull's margin recovery thesis work. And that's precisely why it's a risk, not a confirmed outcome.

Here's the critical question embedded in the "third path": **at what revenue level does TXN's demand genuinely outpace production capacity, causing inventory to normalize at the rate required for margin recovery?**

The math matters here. At $4.83B quarterly revenue and ~58% gross margins, TXN is generating $2.8B in gross profit per quarter. To reach 65% gross margins at $5.5B quarterly revenue (a reasonable FY2027 bull case), gross profit would be $3.575B — a $775M quarterly improvement. That requires both revenue growth **and** margin expansion simultaneously. The margin expansion requires utilization to improve meaningfully. And utilization improves when demand genuinely outstrips production — which requires inventory to have already normalized enough that TXN is shipping primarily from current production rather than existing stock.

Do you see the circularity? Margins recover when demand outpaces production capacity. Demand outpaces production capacity when inventory normalizes. Inventory normalizes when margins have already compressed production to below current demand levels. **This is a self-reinforcing cycle that requires the initial condition — a genuine demand surge above production levels — to trigger.** That surge has not happened yet. Q1's $100M inventory reduction is not a surge. It's a tick.

The bull's "third path" is real but unconfirmed, and the timeline for confirmation extends well into FY2027. You're paying 32x forward earnings today for a thesis that requires FY2027 execution to validate.
