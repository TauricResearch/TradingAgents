## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**Texas Instruments Incorporated (TXN)** is one of the world's largest semiconductor companies, headquartered in Dallas, Texas. TXN designs and manufactures analog integrated circuits (ICs) and embedded processors, serving a broad array of industrial, automotive, personal electronics, communications, and enterprise computing markets. The company is widely regarded as a premier analog and embedded processing franchise with durable competitive advantages built on:

- Proprietary manufacturing (300mm wafer fab capacity)
- Decades of IP and engineering expertise
- Deep customer relationships and a broad portfolio of ~80,000 products
- A disciplined capital allocation strategy emphasizing long-term free cash flow per share growth

TXN operates primarily through two segments:
1. **Analog** (~75% of revenues) — power management, signal chain, motor drivers
2. **Embedded Processing** (~15% of revenues) — microcontrollers and processors
3. **Other** (~10%) — DLP products and calculators

- **Gross PPE:** Grew from $9.95B (FY2022) to $17.68B (FY2025) — a $7.7B addition

- **CapEx is decelerating:** From -$5.07B (FY2023) → -$4.82B (FY2024) → -$4.55B (FY2025) → Q1 2026 run-rate of ~$2.7B annualized — a powerful FCF tailwind building

## 9. ⚠️ Risk Factors

1. **Cyclicality:** The semiconductor industry is inherently cyclical. Revenue dropped ~22% from FY2022 peak to FY2024 trough.
2. **Geopolitical/Trade Risk:** U.S.-China trade tensions and export control regulations represent material risks to TXN's China revenues (~20%+ of total).
3. **Debt Load:** Total debt at $14B is elevated versus pre-2022 levels, though manageable given cash generation capacity.
4. **Inventory Risk:** Industry-wide inventory corrections can suppress pricing and volumes rapidly.
5. **CapEx Execution Risk:** New fab ramps carry yield and timing risk, potentially delaying the FCF recovery thesis.
6. **High Valuation:** TTM P/E of 49.5x is stretched; the bull case depends heavily on forward EPS recovery.
