## 4. 🔍 Sector & Macro Context

### Why Semiconductors Matter Now:
The semiconductor sector is at the epicenter of several structural megatrends:

1. **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Demand for GPUs, AI accelerators (e.g., NVIDIA H100/B100 series, AMD MI300), and specialized chips has exploded. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) are spending hundreds of billions on AI infrastructure through 2026+.

2. **Data Center Expansion:** Global data center capex reached record levels in 2025-2026, driving demand for high-bandwidth memory (HBM), advanced logic chips, and power semiconductors.

3. **Geopolitical Risk:** U.S.-China semiconductor export controls (including restrictions on advanced chips and lithography equipment) remain a persistent risk to revenues of SOXX holdings with meaningful China exposure (e.g., Qualcomm, Applied Materials, Lam Research).

4. **Cyclicality:** The semiconductor industry is inherently cyclical. As of mid-2026, the industry appears to be in a **mid-to-late upcycle** based on the 50-day vs. 200-day MA gap and proximity to 52-week highs.

5. **Inventory Correction Tail-End:** After the 2022-2023 inventory glut, the sector largely normalized by 2024-2025, enabling a strong earnings recovery cycle.

### Representative SOXX Holdings (Typical):
SOXX typically concentrates in leading semiconductor names, including:
- **NVIDIA (NVDA)** — AI/Data Center GPU dominance
- **Broadcom (AVGO)** — Custom ASICs, networking chips
