## 1. Company Overview & Profile

**Credo Technology Group Holding Ltd (CRDO)** is a high-performance connectivity semiconductor company headquartered in San Jose, California. Credo designs and sells proprietary, power-efficient Serializer/Deserializer (SerDes) solutions, Active Electrical Cables (AECs), line-card retimers, and optical digital signal processors (DSPs). Its products serve hyperscaler data centers, cloud operators, and large-scale AI infrastructure buildouts — precisely the secular megatrends driving the most intense semiconductor demand in recent years.

Key business facts:
- **Market Cap:** ~$40.1 billion (as of June 4, 2026)
- **Beta:** 3.18 — highly volatile, moves ~3x the broader market
- **52-Week Range:** $66.75 – $245.95, reflecting extreme growth-driven momentum

## 6. Key Risks & Considerations

1. **Customer Concentration Risk:** CRDO is known to derive a very large portion of revenue from a small number of hyperscale customers (notably Microsoft). Losing or seeing slowdown from a key customer would be materially adverse.

2. **Inventory Build Risk:** $208M in inventory (particularly $169M in finished goods) creates potential write-down risk if demand decelerates.

3. **Share Dilution:** SBC running at ~$52M/quarter and a $351.7M equity raise increase diluted share count significantly. Diluted shares grew from ~168M to ~192M in one year.

4. **High Valuation Sensitivity:** At ~119x TTM P/E, any guidance miss or macro headwind could result in severe de-rating. Forward P/E of 25x requires continued explosive earnings growth.

5. **Competitive Landscape:** Competition from Marvell, Broadcom, Inphi (now part of Marvell), and InnoLink in the SerDes/AEC market is intense. New entrants targeting AI networking could disrupt CRDO's advantage.

6. **Acquisition Integration:** The emergence of ~$88.5M in goodwill/intangibles suggests an acquisition occurred in mid-FY2026. Integration risk and any potential impairment should be tracked.

7. **AI Capex Dependency:** CRDO's growth trajectory is deeply tied to hyperscaler AI infrastructure spending. Any slowdown in data center buildouts would disproportionately impact revenues.
