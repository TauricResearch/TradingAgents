The aggressive analyst's most important concession is decisive here: the $164 stop manages maximum loss but NOT the silent erosion of expected value while price stays above it (e.g., a two-notch downgrade plus seasonally weak Q2 cash flow pushing the stock to $175 without firing the stop). The Anthropic stake is real embedded optionality but is illiquid, of uncertain ownership percentage after dilution, and its IPO-stage mark depends on a multiple sensitive to the current adverse macro—optionality, not a near-term catalyst. The overhead supply between $210–265 and the unreclaimed 200-day MA (~$220) confirm this is a recovery within a larger downtrend, not yet a confirmed structural reversal.
