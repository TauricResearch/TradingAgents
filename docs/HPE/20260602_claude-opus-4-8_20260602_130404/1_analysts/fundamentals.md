## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company (HPE) is a global technology leader spun off from Hewlett-Packard in November 2015. HPE operates across enterprise IT infrastructure segments including servers, storage, networking, hybrid cloud, and intelligent edge solutions. Its key business lines include:

- **Compute / Servers** — Industry-standard and high-performance computing servers
- **Storage** — All-flash and software-defined storage solutions
- **Intelligent Edge (Aruba)** — Networking, connectivity, and edge computing
- **Hybrid Cloud / GreenLake** — As-a-service cloud consumption models
- **Financial Services** — Financing and technology lifecycle management

A pivotal strategic event was the **acquisition of Juniper Networks**, which closed in Q3 FY2025 (July 2025), significantly expanding HPE's networking capabilities and adding substantial scale — visible in the sharp jump in assets, revenue, and debt from Q3 FY2025 onward.

## 9. ⚠️ Key Risks

1. **Leverage Risk:** Net debt of ~$16.8B and D/E of ~87x are elevated post-Juniper. Rising rates could increase interest costs materially. Interest paid in FY2025 was $1.018B.
2. **Goodwill Impairment Risk:** $23.8B in goodwill + $6.1B in intangibles represent ~40% of total assets. Any deterioration in networking/Juniper performance could trigger further impairments.
3. **Integration Execution Risk:** Juniper integration is complex. FY2025 saw $1.6B in impairments suggesting some integration challenges.
4. **FCF/Dividend Coverage:** FY2025 FCF of $627M was insufficient to cover dividends of $796M. Improvement in FY2026 FCF is critical.
5. **Macro & Tariff Sensitivity:** As a global hardware/networking company, HPE is exposed to supply chain disruptions, trade tariffs, and enterprise IT capex cycles.
6. **Negative Tangible Book Value:** -$5.15B tangible book signals asset-lite valuation reliance on intangibles — vulnerable to impairment in downturns.
