## 7. ⚠️ Key Risks & Considerations

1. **Elevated D/E Ratio (53.8):** Headline debt-to-equity appears very high but is distorted by massive treasury stock balances. Net debt is falling rapidly and interest coverage is extremely comfortable.
2. **High Beta (1.548):** NFLX stock is significantly more volatile than the market. Macro or sector-level risk events can produce outsized drawdowns.
3. **Q1 2026 Non-Recurring Interest Income:** The $2.59B interest income in Q1 2026 (vs. ~$40-51M typically per quarter) is anomalous and inflated reported net income. Traders should normalize earnings for this item.
4. **Goodwill & Intangibles:** $33.4B in goodwill/intangibles (mainly content assets and acquisition-related) represents ~55% of total assets; any impairment would meaningfully impact book value.
5. **Content Amortization Burden:** D&A was $4.3B in Q1 2026 alone, driven primarily by content amortization ($4.22B). This is a recurring, heavy non-cash expense that depresses GAAP earnings and masks underlying cash generation.
6. **Share Price Near 52-Week Lows:** Current trading well below the 52-week high of $134 (50-day avg ~$92.51) may reflect broader market concerns or valuation re-rating. The technical picture merits caution.
7. **Competition:** Intensifying competition from Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, Apple TV+, and emerging global platforms continues to pressure subscriber acquisition costs.
