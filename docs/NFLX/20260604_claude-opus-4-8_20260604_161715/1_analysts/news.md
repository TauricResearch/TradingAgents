## 4. 🍁 Canadian Regulatory Reversal — Near-Term Catalyst

### What Happened
- Canada's broadcast/communications regulator (CRTC equivalent) had issued a ruling requiring **large U.S. streamers to contribute 15% of Canadian revenue to Canadian content funds**.
- The **Motion Picture Association** and the **U.S. Ambassador to Canada** lobbied aggressively against this policy.
- Late Wednesday (approximately June 3), the **Canadian government reversed course**, directing the regulator to **rescind the requirement**.

### Market Impact
- This reversal was the **direct catalyst** for NFLX snapping its eight-day losing streak, with shares rising the following day.
- The policy, had it stood, would have materially impacted margins on Canadian revenue — a non-trivial market for a global platform with millions of Canadian subscribers.
- The reversal removes a **near-term regulatory overhang** and signals that the U.S.-Canada content trade relationship remains navigable.
