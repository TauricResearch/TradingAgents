So where does that leave us? The bull is probably right on a multi-year view of the business. The bear is probably right that *at $85.85, in a confirmed downtrend, at consensus fair value, ahead of a binary earnings event*, the risk/reward is not the slam-dunk asymmetry the bull claims. Neither side earned a high-conviction directional call: the bull's edge is the franchise quality, the bear's edge is the timing and the absence of valuation margin of safety. That genuine balance — strong business, poor entry — is exactly the situation Hold is meant for. I'm not going to manufacture conviction the debate didn't produce.
