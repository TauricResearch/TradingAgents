But the bear did NOT win the investment argument. His entire case is, by his own framing, a timing overlay on a business both sides agree is excellent and reasonably valued. "Wait for confirmation" systematically means paying a higher multiple after someone else takes the early risk, and the bear never produced evidence of fundamental impairment — only price action and macro sentiment. When a best-in-class compounder trades at a reasonable multiple with a defined risk level and a near-term catalyst, the disciplined answer isn't to sit it out entirely; it's to build the position with respect for the downside path. That's why I'm landing on Overweight rather than a full Buy — I'm honoring the bear's legitimate timing and technical concerns by scaling in rather than backing up the truck, but I'm not willing to sit on the sidelines on a 9.4% timing bet against a business of this quality.
