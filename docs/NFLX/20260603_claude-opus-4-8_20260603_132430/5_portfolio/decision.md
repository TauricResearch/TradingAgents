**Rating**: Overweight

**Executive Summary**: Initiate a scaled long position in NFLX at ~$83 with a 25% initial tranche, reserving the majority of capital for disciplined accumulation. Set a thesis-invalidating stop at a decisive high-volume break below $74. Deploy a second ~30% tranche into the $75–77 zone only on a confirmed volume-reversal signal, and the final ~45% on post-July 15 catalyst confirmation or a Bollinger middle-band reclaim (~$87.40) with positive MACD. This is a 12–24 month overweight predicated on price reconciling upward to an accelerating, reasonably valued, best-in-class business.

**Investment Thesis**: The debate produced a striking consensus: all three analysts agreed NFLX is a genuinely exceptional business — FCF grew from $1.62B (2022) to $9.46B (2025), operating margins expanded for four consecutive years (17.8% to 32.3%), net debt fell 73% in one year toward a net-cash position, and the ad tier represents real, early-stage advertising-platform optionality. The entire disagreement collapsed to entry timing and sizing, not business quality. That is the defining feature of this analysis and the reason a sidelined Hold is unjustified.

On valuation, the aggressive analyst's $20B run-rate FCF figure was correctly dismantled — the conservative and neutral analysts showed Q1 2026 included a non-recurring $2.85B interest-income windfall, leaving normalized run-rate FCF near $10–12B and a normalized multiple closer to 31x FCF / ~21x forward earnings. That is a reasonable-but-not-extreme premium for a dominant platform, which argues against maximum aggression (40% initial tranche) but equally against the conservative analyst's near-negligible 10–15% placeholder.

The bear's strongest surviving points are genuine and I am not dismissing them: (1) the June 2 MACD histogram failure after a ten-session recovery attempt is real new information — the market explicitly rejected a recovery, and direction/persistence carry signal even if magnitude (-0.148) is modest; (2) declining ATR in a downtrend signals orderly distribution, not capitulation; (3) the 2026 World Cup is hosted in North America, Netflix's highest-ARPU cohort, embedding a specific, unmodeled engagement risk directly in the July 15 print's quarter; (4) beta of 1.55 against active geopolitical shock vectors (Strait of Hormuz, energy prices) means a macro risk-off event could gap the stock through the stop. The conservative analyst's own probability estimate of 55–65% for a test toward $75–77 before recovery is credible and directly shapes sizing.

But the bear never produced evidence of fundamental impairment — only price action, macro sentiment, and timing. His 2022 analogy fails because that selloff coincided with an actual subscriber-loss crisis and Fed rate hikes; today subscribers are 325M and growing, FCF is at a record, and the ad tier did not exist then. His $75 buy target — just below current levels — is itself an admission this business is worth owning very near here. The conservative analyst's 10–15% placeholder also creates the worst structural outcome he claims to avoid: if the catalyst fires without a prior dip (his own 35–45% scenario), he must chase 85–90% of the position into a $95–98 gap against the declining 50 SMA, producing a terrible blended cost.

The neutral analyst's synthesis is the most evidentially honest landing spot: a 25% initial tranche participates meaningfully in the upside without front-running into the active distribution signal; a mandatory volume-reversal filter on the $75–77 tranche elegantly resolves the accumulation-vs-distribution dispute (if institutions are quietly accumulating, the volume confirmation appears; if it is pure distribution, the tranche never deploys); and reserving the largest tranche for post-catalyst confirmation deploys maximum capital at the highest-conviction entry. This honors the bear's legitimate technical and macro concerns through sizing rather than abstention — the correct response to a best-in-class compounder at a reasonable multiple with a defined risk level and a near-term catalyst.
