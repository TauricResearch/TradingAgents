**Recommendation**: Underweight

**Rationale**: Alright, let me walk you through how I'm reading this one, because both analysts were genuinely sharp and this wasn't a blowout in either direction.

The bull's structural case is real and I don't want to dismiss it: ARM is the architectural substrate of the AI computing era. Every AWS Graviton, Google Axion, Azure Cobalt, NVIDIA AI PC, premium smartphone, and automotive ADAS chip pays ARM a royalty. The ARMv9 royalty rate uplift (~2x ARMv8) is a mechanical revenue tailwind that requires no new customer wins. Gross margins of 97.6%, a clean balance sheet with $3.6B cash and no financial debt, 22.5% revenue CAGR, and operating margins expanding from ~0% to 29.5% in two years are all genuinely high-quality attributes. The moat — 30 years of software ecosystem, toolchains, and developer familiarity — is not something RISC-V replicates in the relevant horizon for premium mobile and data center. I fully credit that the business is excellent and the secular AI tailwind is real and confirmed (Computex, hyperscaler custom silicon, AI PCs).

But here's where the debate actually turned, and it's the bear who landed the decisive blows — and critically, it was the BULL'S OWN analytics that did the damage. When pressed on valuation, the bull ran a DCF on his own optimistic-but-reasonable assumptions (25% EPS growth for 5 years, 50x terminal multiple, 10% discount) and arrived at roughly $194 per share against a $411.83 price. He then ran a probability-weighted scenario analysis using the bear's weights and got $364 — still below the current price. Even under his own probability weights he only got to ~$449, a slim premium. When an analyst's own models keep printing numbers at or below the market price, and the rebuttal is "trust the narrative" and "you don't sell structural winners 12% above fair value," that's a tell. The bull effectively conceded the valuation case and pivoted to story.

The bear also won several specific exchanges on the merits: (1) the 0.23% FCF yield — below T-bills — with economic FCF arguably negative after $1.052B of SBC; (2) ARM's SBC intensity at 21.4% of revenue is a genuine outlier vs. NVIDIA (~3-4%), MSFT (~5-6%), GOOG (~8-9%), and it re-accelerated in absolute terms in FY26 rather than normalizing; (3) the FTC probe into the ATA/Compute Subsystems bundling model is a real overhang — and the bear's point that ATA is the *mechanism* for future royalty-rate renegotiation (the very thing the 128x multiple is pricing) is sharper than the bull's "only 4-6% of revenue" rebuttal; (4) the China RISC-V S-curve with unlimited state backing is an early-innings displacement risk that is, by definition, not yet visible in the data, so "already priced in / growing through it" is complacent.

Where the bull genuinely scored: the SBC double-counting nuance is legitimate (diluted forward EPS does capture share-count dilution), the FTC has a high exclusionary-conduct bar with RISC-V as a live alternative, RISC-V really is concentrated in low-royalty embedded/budget segments today, and the CapEx surge is more plausibly a chip-design capability investment than a business-model change. These keep this from being a Sell.

Net: this is a great business at a demanding price with multiple live, unquantifiable risks (FTC, China, SBC) and a parabolic technical setup (RSI 81, ~92% above the 50-day, declining volume, $10M July put hedges). The asymmetry from $411.83 is unfavorable — the stock was at $100 four months ago, which underscores how violently it can reprice. Even the bull repeatedly told us $411 is "not the optimal entry" and to wait for $340-360. When the most bullish voice in the room is telling you to wait for a 17% pullback, and his own DCF says fair value is well below spot, the honest synthesis is caution. I'm not calling for a full exit because the secular franchise quality is real and a Sell would overweight worst-case scenarios — but trimming into this momentum extension and demanding a much better entry is the disciplined call. Hence Underweight rather than Hold or Sell.

Set buy alerts at the $340-360 zone (10-day EMA / first meaningful retracement); only consider re-adding the trimmed portion if fundamentals (next quarter's royalty growth, geographic/China mix, FTC developments) confirm the thesis is intact at that lower level.

4) HEDGE TAIL RISK: For the retained core, consider modest protective puts or a put spread (3-month tenor) to guard against an FTC headline, a China revenue disclosure, or a guidance miss — any of which could gap the stock down sharply given the demanding 128x forward multiple and 0.23% FCF yield.

5) MONITOR THE DECISIVE VARIABLES: Track (a) FTC ATA/bundling investigation progress — a consent decree affecting bundling would impair the royalty-rate-expansion thesis; (b) China geographic revenue trend and RISC-V adoption signals (UNISOC, Huawei ecosystem); (c) SBC as % of revenue and diluted share count trajectory; (d) CapEx — whether it normalizes or keeps accelerating; (e) quarterly royalty revenue and ARMv9 mix. Material deterioration in two or more shifts the rating toward Sell; clear de-risking (FTC resolution, China stabilization, SBC normalization, multiple compression to a defensible level) shifts it back toward Overweight.

Sizing guidance: net portfolio weight should sit modestly below benchmark/neutral, consistent with Underweight — enough to retain optionality on a best-in-class AI infrastructure franchise, but reduced enough that an adverse repricing toward the bull's own ~$194 DCF or the recent $100 low does limited damage.
