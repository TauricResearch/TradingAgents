The Qualcomm analog the bull chose cuts against him on magnitude — a documented ~45% peak-to-trough drawdown during the proceeding, and ARM would still be ~260x trailing at that trough, meaning no obvious value floor.

So why Underweight and not outright Sell? Because the bear's case is fundamentally a price-and-timing argument against an excellent business, and the strongest disagreement is over probability weights, not facts. A full Sell would imply conviction the business itself is impaired or the thesis is broken — neither side argued that, and the structural growth story is genuinely strong. The honest read is: the company deserves to be owned at the right price; the current price embeds near-flawless execution with meaningful downside if any of FTC, China, SBC, or macro disappoints. That asymmetry — limited near-term upside above an already-stretched price versus a credible 30-45% drawdown path — warrants trimming exposure and waiting for a better entry, not abandoning the name entirely. That's Underweight.
