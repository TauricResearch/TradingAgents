## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Micron Technology, Inc. (ticker: **MU**) is one of the world's leading semiconductor companies, specializing in the design and manufacture of memory and storage solutions. Its primary product lines include:

- **DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory):** The dominant global product category including DDR5, HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI/data center applications.
- **NAND Flash:** Storage memory used in SSDs, enterprise storage, and mobile devices.
- **NOR Flash & Other Embedded Memory:** For specialized applications.

Micron operates globally with manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Japan, Singapore, Taiwan, and China. As a capital-intensive cyclical semiconductor company, Micron's financial performance is historically tied to memory pricing cycles — but with the AI-driven HBM supercycle, Micron is experiencing a profound secular demand upgrade.

## 7. 🌐 Strategic Factors & Competitive Position

### HBM/AI Tailwinds
- Micron is one of only three suppliers of HBM globally (alongside Samsung and SK Hynix). HBM3E and forthcoming HBM4 are critical for NVIDIA GPUs, AMD accelerators, and custom AI chips (Google TPU, AWS Trainium).
- Demand for HBM far exceeds supply, creating exceptional pricing power. Micron has reportedly **sold out HBM capacity through 2025-2026**.

### CHIPS Act Investment
- Micron secured **~$6.1B in federal grants** under the U.S. CHIPS and Science Act to build new advanced memory fabs in **Boise, Idaho** and **Clay, New York** — a massive long-term catalyst for domestic supply chain positioning.

### Cyclical Risk Awareness
- Despite the current boom, memory is inherently cyclical. NAND oversupply risks remain a watchpoint. The FY2023 loss cycle (-$5.83B) is a sobering reminder of downside potential.

### Capital Intensity
- CapEx of ~$6.4B per quarter is among the highest in the semiconductor sector. Execution on new fabs and technology transitions (1-beta DRAM, 232-layer NAND+, HBM4) will be critical.
