## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

Micron Technology, Inc. (NASDAQ: **MU**) is one of the world's largest semiconductor manufacturers, specializing in **DRAM (Dynamic Random-Access Memory), NAND flash memory, and NOR flash memory** products. Founded in 1978 and headquartered in Boise, Idaho, Micron serves a global customer base spanning data centers, mobile, automotive, industrial, and consumer electronics end-markets.

Micron is a critical player in the AI infrastructure buildout, particularly through its **High Bandwidth Memory (HBM)** chips used in AI accelerators. The company competes directly with Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix in DRAM, and with Samsung, Kioxia, and Western Digital in NAND.

## 8. ⚠️ Risk Factors

1. **Cyclicality:** The memory semiconductor industry is notoriously cyclical (as evidenced by the -$5.83B net loss in FY2023). Any demand slowdown or pricing correction could rapidly compress margins.
2. **HBM Concentration Risk:** A substantial portion of current outperformance is tied to HBM demand for AI training. Any slowdown in AI capex could disproportionately impact MU.
3. **Geopolitical Risk:** Micron has significant operations and revenue exposure to China; U.S.-China trade tensions and potential export restrictions remain a persistent overhang.
4. **Competition:** Samsung and SK Hynix are also aggressively ramping HBM capacity; competitive supply additions could erode pricing power.
5. **CapEx Intensity:** With quarterly CapEx running at $5-7B, any revenue shortfall could quickly flip FCF negative.
6. **Accounts Receivable Surge:** AR jumped from $8.01B (Nov 2025) to $15.39B (Feb 2026) — a $7.4B increase in a single quarter. This is an unusually large jump and warrants monitoring for collectibility risk.
7. **Debt-to-Equity Ratio:** At 14.9, the D/E remains elevated even as it declines; however, the absolute debt level is falling rapidly.
