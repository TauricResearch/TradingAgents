## The "Re-Rating vs. Overshoot" Framework — Let's Actually Define the Terms

The bull's final line is their most important argument: this is a re-rating of what Micron *is*, not a temporary overshoot. And I want to engage with this seriously because it's the most important question in the entire debate.

A **re-rating** is permanent multiple expansion driven by a structural change in a business's competitive position, earnings durability, or addressable market. It produces a new, higher "fair value" multiple that doesn't revert to prior norms.

An **overshoot** is temporary multiple expansion driven by narrative momentum, cycle enthusiasm, or institutional FOMO, which eventually reverts as the catalyst loses novelty.

How do you tell them apart in real time? Here's the test I'd apply:

**Question 1:** Are the incremental earnings being generated by a product with durable competitive advantages that *didn't exist* in prior cycles? 

Partial yes. HBM is real. But HBM is also being produced by SK Hynix and will be produced by Samsung. "Durable competitive advantage" in a product where two well-funded competitors are equally capable is a stretch.

**Question 2:** Is the new earnings base protected by switching costs, network effects, or intellectual property that prevents competitors from eroding it over a 3-5 year horizon?

This is where the bull's case weakens considerably. HBM is not a software platform with switching costs. It is a physical component with open specifications that hyperscalers can and do multi-source. NVIDIA qualifies multiple HBM suppliers specifically to avoid dependence on any single vendor. That's not a re-rating moat. That's a commodifying market structure.

**Question 3:** Is the new multiple being applied to sustainable earnings, or to peak-cycle earnings?

The bull cannot answer "sustainable" without assuming 70%+ gross margins persist indefinitely — which contradicts the very nature of the product they're describing as their competitive moat.

The test for "re-rating" fails on at least two of three criteria. What we're more likely looking at is a **partial re-rating** — Micron is genuinely structurally better than it was — combined with a significant **cyclical overshoot** of the magnitude that the current price implies. Partial re-rating plus significant overshoot equals: **fair value somewhere in the $700-900 range, with current price representing 15-50% premium to that fair value depending on how durable you believe the margins are.**
