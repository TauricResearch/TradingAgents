3. The bear's strongest and most defensible point by the end was the risk/reward at the *specific entry price* of $236. Daily TD-9 complete, weekly TD-9 at -8/9, RSI divergence, price ~33% above the 50-day SMA, heading into a binary earnings event on a 1.655 Beta name. The setup genuinely is asymmetric to the downside in the near term, and the $210-$220 zone is well-supported.

Here's the synthesis the debate actually points to: the bull won the *thesis*, the bear won the *near-term entry timing*. When the destination is up but the near-term path has legitimate exhaustion signals and a binary catalyst, the disciplined call isn't "back up the truck at $236" and it isn't "sell a 21% grower with a database moat and a $290 institutional target." It's a constructive, build-the-position-on-weakness stance. That's Overweight, not Buy and not Hold — Hold would understate how strong and uncontested the fundamental engine is, while a full Buy would ignore the genuine FCF/leverage risk and the very real technical exhaustion the bear documented. I'm giving the edge to the bull because he never lost the fundamental argument, but I'm respecting the bear's timing discipline in how we size and stage the entry.
