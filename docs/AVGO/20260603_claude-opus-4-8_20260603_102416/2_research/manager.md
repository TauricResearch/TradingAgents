That's a timing/sizing argument, not a thesis-breaking one. The bull never adequately answered the certainty-premium compression risk or the fact that a robust beat is already priced into a 14% pre-earnings run. Both sides essentially conceded the destination; they disagreed on the entry. That asymmetry — strong structural case, weak near-term entry case — is exactly what an Overweight (not a Buy, not a Hold) is designed to express. I'm constructive and want exposure increased over time, but I am NOT endorsing a full-conviction entry at maximum extension into tonight's print. The bear earned enough on timing to pull me off "Buy," but the bull earned far too much on the structural compounding case for me to sit at "Hold" and pretend the evidence is balanced.
