## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**Broadcom Inc. (AVGO)** is a global technology leader in the design, development, and supply of a broad range of semiconductor, infrastructure software, and networking solutions. Broadcom's portfolio spans data center networking, custom AI accelerators (XPUs), broadband, wireless, storage, and enterprise infrastructure software. The company serves hyperscalers, cloud providers, enterprise customers, and telecom operators globally.

Broadcom has aggressively expanded through acquisitions — most notably the **$61 billion acquisition of VMware** (closed November 2023), which dramatically transformed the company into a hybrid hardware/software giant. This acquisition more than doubled its revenue base and reshaped its financial profile.

## 11. 🎯 Actionable Insights for Traders

1. **AI Revenue Ramp is Real and Accelerating**: The 29.5% YoY quarterly revenue growth is not a one-time event — it reflects deepening custom silicon relationships with hyperscalers and growing VMware enterprise software revenues.

2. **Forward Earnings Tell the True Story**: With GAAP TTM P/E at 65.7x but Forward P/E at ~20.5x, the stock's apparent overvaluation is largely an artifact of amortization. Traders who focus only on TTM GAAP may be misled.

3. **FCF is the Core Valuation Driver**: At a ~$29B annualized FCF run rate and $1.86T market cap, the FCF yield is approximately **1.6%**, rising to potentially **2–2.5%** as AI revenues scale — reasonable for a dominant platform with secular tailwinds.

4. **Deleverage Story Provides Upside Catalyst**: Net debt declining from $57.8B to $51.9B in just three quarters demonstrates rapid debt paydown. As debt falls, financial flexibility for more buybacks/dividends grows, supporting EPS accretion.

5. **Massive Q1 FY2026 Buyback ($7.85B)** is a strong insider confidence signal. This single quarter's buyback exceeded the entire FY2023 capital expenditure budget.

6. **Deferred Revenue (~$12.9B)** represents locked-in future revenue from VMware subscription contracts, providing a high degree of revenue visibility.

7. **Watch Amortization Cliff**: As the $30B in remaining intangible assets amortize over the next 3–5 years, GAAP EPS will improve materially, potentially compressing the TTM P/E toward the forward multiple.
