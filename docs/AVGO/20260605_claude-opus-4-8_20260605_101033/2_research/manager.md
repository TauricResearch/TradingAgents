**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: Alright, here's how I read this debate after watching both sides go ten rounds on AVGO.

The bull's core case is strong on substance: this is a genuinely world-class business. ~$75B revenue growing 20%+ YoY, operating margins near 49%, $12.9B in contracted VMware deferred revenue, Google's $85B capex commitment flowing through the order book, and a deleveraging story that's real (net debt down ~$6B over three quarters). The hyperscaler custom-silicon and networking positions (Tomahawk/Jericho, SerDes IP) are legitimate moats, and the bull made a fair point that networking actually gets MORE valuable as clusters scale. Critically, the bull also won the technical/valuation framing on a few points: covenant risk is essentially a non-issue at ~13x interest coverage and ~1.24x net debt/EBITDA, and the FY2024 precedent showed the market looks through GAAP/amortization noise.

But — and this is what kept me from a full Buy — the bear landed real, conceded damage. Over the course of the debate the bull was forced to walk back from "AVGO is cheap" to "AVGO is appropriately valued." That migration matters. By the end, the bull conceded: (1) true shareholder FCF is ~$20-21B net of $8.7B SBC, not the $29B headline he anchored to for six rounds; (2) the post-revision forward P/E is more like 21-24x, the historical midpoint, not the "cheap" 20.5x; (3) the monthly TD-9 sell setup is "the strongest technical argument in the bear's case"; and (4) the AI revenue miss — on the single line that justifies the premium — deserves genuine follow-up. The bear's sharpest logical trap also stuck: if revenue-recognition timing is unpredictable enough to explain the Q2 miss, then the forward EPS anchor underpinning the whole valuation is correspondingly less reliable.

So where does that leave us? This is NOT a balanced Hold situation — the bull clearly has the stronger structural, multi-year thesis, and "appropriately valued world-class compounder at the low-to-mid end of its historical multiple after a 15%+ drawdown" is a constructive setup, not a neutral one. But the bear won the TIMING argument convincingly. The honest read is that the strongest near-term risks (unquantified margin compression, building monthly TD-9, untested 50 SMA, rate-hike macro, sector-wide AI derisking) argue against backing up the truck today at $418, while the strongest long-term arguments (FCF engine, AI/networking moat, hyperscaler capex, deleveraging) argue against sitting it out entirely and missing the compounding.

That asymmetry — strong business, imperfect entry — is exactly what Overweight is for: lean in, build constructively, but scale in with discipline rather than committing full size into an unconfirmed bottom. I'm not going to Buy because the bull himself conceded the valuation is "appropriate not cheap" and the dominant-timeframe technical is bearish. I'm not going to Hold because the fundamental case is too strong and the stock has already absorbed meaningful multiple compression. Overweight, accumulated on a scale-in plan, is the right call.
